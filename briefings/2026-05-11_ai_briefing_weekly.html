OPENAI 5 May 2026
OpenAI replaces the ChatGPT default with GPT-5.5 Instant — terser, fewer hallucinations, fewer emoji
GPT-5.5 Instant rolls out as the new default for all ChatGPT users and is available via the API as chat-latest. OpenAI's internal evals report 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts (medical/legal/financial) and ~30% shorter outputs versus GPT-5.3 Instant. Plus and Pro tiers also get cross-conversation, file, and Gmail memory recall on the web. Treat the hallucination delta cautiously — it's a vendor benchmark, not third-party.

Policy & macro · 1 story
EU 7 May 2026
EU Council and Parliament agree to simplify the AI Act and delay parts of it — the much-discussed pause is real
The provisional agreement under the "Omnibus VII" digital simplification package walks back several timelines. The Commission's proposal allows high-risk AI rules to start applying up to 16 months later, contingent on standards and tools being in place. Nudification apps get an explicit ban as a sweetener. The 2 August 2026 hard deadline for full applicability isn't dead — but the high-risk obligations that were the sharpest commercial constraint just got real breathing room. For anyone with a deployment plan keyed to August, re-read the obligations.

💡 EDITOR'S VIEW
1.   The most actionable shift this week is Anthropic-in-Microsoft-365. Until last week, the assumption was that Microsoft would defend its Office stack with Copilot. The Anthropic add-ins re-open the question of whether Microsoft is willing to be neutral plumbing for whichever model wins on quality — which would change every enterprise AI procurement conversation in the next 6 months.
2.   The two simultaneous default-model swaps (Opus 4.7 GA, GPT-5.5 Instant) are best read together: both labs are quietly consolidating around "good enough, faster, fewer hallucinations" rather than benchmark heroics. The era of dramatic model-launch theater is cooling. Expect the next 12 months to be won on distribution, latency, and trust — not raw capability deltas.
3.   The EU's Omnibus VII pivot is the one to actually re-plan around if you have an EU-deployed product. The high-risk timeline shift is real and material; don't wait for the final text to revisit your compliance roadmap.
Wildcard worth naming: the supposed Anthropic compute deal. If primary confirmation lands in the next two weeks, it changes the capacity narrative substantially. If it doesn't, it's a reminder of why aggregator-only items get the yellow banner.
