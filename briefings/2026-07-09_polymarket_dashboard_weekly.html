The board's master variable rotated inside 72 hours: from a July 14 CPI print to a July 8 Trump repudiation. Trump's July 8 repudiation of the Islamabad Memorandum, following a fresh exchange of US-Iran attacks, is the second geopolitical inflection in as many months, and it invalidates the ~$345M UMA-resolved permanent-peace complex on the substance a week after the mechanism was already in dispute. The important structural point: Polymarket's own long-dated geopolitical prices are updating slower than the news, meaning nearly every Iran signal in this edition is a Monday-vintage print reading a Wednesday-broken world. Weight the Fed, cap-leadership, and model-benchmark markets (which resolve mechanically) above the treaty-interpretation and diplomacy markets from here.
