1 New Lead — Series A June 1, 2026 Endra · $50M Series A · $75M total

a16z leads Endra's $50M Series A — AI that automates building MEP design, attacking a $150B+ services market

a16z led a $50M Series A in Endra, the Stockholm-based AI platform that automates the design work of mechanical, electrical and plumbing (MEP) consultants — the engineers who specify the systems inside every building. Notion Capital and Norrsken VC participated; the round brings Endra to $75M raised in under two years. The product generates coordinated MEP designs, calculations and documentation in minutes, work that historically consumed weeks or months inside Revit and AutoCAD. Founders Niklas Lindgren, Anton Juric, David Rydberg and Gustav Hammarlund plan to hire across Stockholm, NYC and San Francisco. (a16z, FINSMES, StartupHub.AI)

The read: this is vertical-AI-eats-services in its purest form — a constrained, regulated, labor-bound professional category (MEP engineering) where capacity is gated by human throughput, and an AI tool that compresses the bottleneck captures the spread. Endra's reported pitch leaned on a data-center-design angle (MEP is the long pole in hyperscaler buildout), which connects it to the AI-infrastructure supercycle a16z is already long. It fits cleanly alongside the firm's other “software automating an old professional vertical” checks. Carded at full confidence: a16z primary announcement plus multiple independent corroborations, named co-investors, dollar figure consistent across sources.

2 New Lead — American Dynamism June 2, 2026 Westmag · $11M seed · out of stealth

a16z leads Westmag's $11M seed to reshore US drone-motor and robot-actuator manufacturing

a16z led an $11M seed in Westmag, which emerged from stealth on June 2 to build domestic US production of the electric motors and actuators that give drones and robots motion — components long dependent on foreign (largely Chinese) supply. Founders Fund and Lux Capital joined. The company is already running a semi-automated line in South San Francisco and ramping to fulfill committed orders for “hundreds of thousands of units” for defense and robotics customers. Founders David Hansen (motor technology) and Jordan Sanders (operations) are riding a hard regulatory tailwind: the FCC's December 2025 ban on foreign-made drone components. (a16z, BusinessWire, Ventureburn)

This is the American Dynamism book waking up after a public dormancy since the January Anduril Series H. Westmag is a textbook expression of the thesis: a single-point-of-failure in the defense/robotics supply chain (motors), a policy mandate forcing reshoring, and committed demand from both defense and the humanoid-robotics wave. The a16z + Founders Fund + Lux trio is the standard hard-tech-defense syndicate. Note the dual relevance — this also feeds the robotics-hardware thesis behind a16z's Mind Robotics co-lead (March), since actuators are the choke point for humanoids too. Full confidence: a16z primary, BusinessWire release, named co-investors.

a16z led a $35M Series A in Lassie, an AI platform aimed at “reluctant operators” — small-business owners buried in back-office work (billing, HR, claims, recruiting). Its first vertical is dental practices. Total raised is now $47M. The round drew a notable operator-angel list: Rahul Vohra (Superhuman), Zach Perret (Plaid), Taavet Hinrikus (Wise), Gokul Rajaram, and Brian Balfour (Reforge), plus Night Capital.
