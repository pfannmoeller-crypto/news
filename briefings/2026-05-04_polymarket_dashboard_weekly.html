Fed monetary path

Macro · 4 underlying markets
trending_downMore hawkish — 0 cuts now dominant at 56%

The biggest single-week move across all seven clusters. The FOMC held at 3.50–3.75% on April 29 and the statement language was more hawkish than expected, citing "resurgent inflation pressures." March PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures) came in hot at 3.5% year-over-year with core CPI at 3.3%. Result: "0 cuts in 2026" surged from 40% to 56%, a +16 point move. The June meeting is priced at 96% no change. Recession held at 24%, essentially flat.

The signal hierarchy is now clear: the Fed will not cut unless inflation decisively breaks. "1 cut (25 basis points)" trails at 19%, meaning the market gives only ~44% total probability to any cut this year. This is a structurally different macro backdrop than two weeks ago — and it has implications for equity pricing, gold, and the geopolitical risk premium.

insightsThe meta-meta read

The Fed has joined Iran as co-master variable; the AI race is pulling away on its own track

The macro complex now has two master variables, not one. Last week Iran was the single key — if Iran de-escalates, all macro dominoes fall green. This week, the Fed's hawkish hold and hot PCE data created a second binding constraint. Even if Iran resolves, the "zero cuts at 56%" pricing means equity multiples face headwinds from rates alone. The macro/geopolitical complex (Iran, Ukraine, Fed, politics, risk) needs both Iran de-escalation AND softer inflation data to move green. One without the other is insufficient.

Within Risk & markets, the Gold/BTC convergence is the week's most interesting micro-signal. Gold went from 53% to 47%, Bitcoin from 32% to 39%. The gap narrowed from 21 points to 8. If this convergence continues, it signals a regime shift from "pure defensive" to "alternative stores of value broadly" — which is incrementally more constructive but still not equity-friendly.

The AI race has fully decoupled from macro. Anthropic's +28pp surge in the May best-model market happened in the same week the Fed went more hawkish and Iran talks stalled. The AI cluster operates on product cycles, benchmark results, and capital formation narratives, not interest rates or geopolitics. Within tech, the Alphabet earnings surprise is the bridge: Alphabet is the one entity that lives in both worlds (macro via ad revenue exposure, AI via Gemini/Cloud).

The architectural read update: Anthropic owns the model layer (more dominantly than last week at 78.5% vs. 50.5%). Nvidia owns the infrastructure layer (but less dominantly at 54% vs. 76.5% for December). Alphabet is emerging as the only dual-layer competitor — both model (Gemini) and infrastructure (Cloud) — and its market-cap probability moved accordingly. The single highest-information event in the next 7 days is the April PCE revision / May employment data window, which tests the Fed thesis, and any Anthropic Claude Mythos general availability announcement, which tests the AI thesis.
