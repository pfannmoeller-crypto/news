Politics & infrastructure · 2 stories
Macro 1 May 2026
 
Pentagon awards classified-AI deals to 7 Big Tech firms — Anthropic blacklisted over safety stance.
 
DoD signed agreements with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, and Reflection to deploy their AI tools across classified networks. Per CNN, Anthropic was excluded after the Trump administration objected to its insistence on safety guardrails for Pentagon use of AI in warfare. This is the first time a frontier lab's safety policy has visibly cost it a flagship US-government contract — worth watching whether it shapes other agencies' procurement or remains a DoD-specific carve-out.

Models & products · 3 stories
Anthropic 30 Apr 2026
 
Anthropic launches Claude Security in public beta — first-party SAST built on Opus 4.7.
 
Claude Security scans entire repos, directories, or branches without API integration — accessible from the Claude.ai sidebar (claude.ai/security) for Enterprise customers. The product reasons over codebases by tracing data flows and component interactions, similar to how a security researcher would. Public beta adds scheduled scans, dismissible findings with documented reasons, and CSV/Markdown exports. The shift here is structural: AI labs are now building their own first-party security products around their models rather than only licensing to incumbents — even as CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, SentinelOne, Trend Micro and Wiz all separately integrate Opus 4.7 into their platforms.
Read more: SecurityWeek · SiliconANGLE · CrowdStrike IR.
 
 
Confidence  ●●●●●  5/5 — Anthropic primary + multiple independent confirmations SecurityWeek
 
Mistral 29 Apr 2026
 
Mistral ships Medium 3.5 — 128B dense, multimodal, open weights at $1.50/M input.
 
Mistral collapses chat, reasoning, and coding into a single 128B dense model with 256k context, configurable reasoning effort per request, and a vision encoder trained from scratch. Reported 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified. Released open weights under a modified MIT license. Paired with two product moves: Vibe (cloud coding agents running long-horizon tasks) and Le Chat Work Mode (parallel multi-step agentic execution across email, calendar, docs, Jira, Slack, with explicit approval gates on sensitive actions). Pricing of $1.50 per million input tokens reinforces the cost-compression trend — for DACH operators wanting an EU-jurisdiction stack, this is the most credible frontier-tier option to date.
Read more: Mistral primary · The Decoder · Hugging Face weights.

xAI 30 Apr 2026
 
Musk testifies under oath that xAI trained Grok partly on OpenAI models.
 
TechCrunch reports Musk's deposition acknowledging that some Grok training relied on OpenAI model outputs. The legal exposure here is narrow but the precedent is wider — distillation from frontier APIs into competing models is now a documented practice at the top of the market, which strengthens OpenAI's footing in any subsequent ToS-violation case and adds pressure to providers' anti-distillation terms.
Read more: TechCrunch.

Enterprise Early May 2026
 
Salesforce announces "agent-native" headless architecture — full platform exposed via APIs.
 
Salesforce is repositioning its platform so AI agents can directly access data, workflows, and tasks without traversing the human UI layer. This is the same direction Mistral's Le Chat Work Mode is pushing — incumbent SaaS rebuilding internals so agents are first-class consumers, not screen-scrapers. For SmartPetPro / Alphapet operations, this matters when picking long-horizon SaaS — agent-native vs. UI-bound vendors will diverge meaningfully on automation cost over the next 12-24 months.

💡 Editor's view
 
  1. Pentagon-Anthropic split is the headline. First time a frontier lab's safety posture demonstrably cost it a flagship US-government contract. Watch whether other agencies follow DoD or whether Anthropic recovers the ground in adjacent civilian contracts. Either way: "safety-first" now has a measurable revenue cost, and competitors will price that into their go-to-market.
  2. Mistral Medium 3.5 is the most credible EU-jurisdiction frontier option to date. 128B dense, open weights, $1.50/M input, native agentic tooling via Le Chat Work Mode. For DACH operators with data-residency or sovereignty constraints, this changes the build/buy math — worth a pilot rather than waiting for the next iteration.
  3. Labs are vertical-integrating into security. Claude Security ships the same week CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, SentinelOne and Wiz announce Opus 4.7 integrations. Anthropic is now both supplier and competitor to its own security partners — a dynamic worth tracking, because it tends to end one of two ways and rarely peacefully.
Wildcard worth naming: GPT-5.5's Bedrock launch + Salesforce going agent-native + Le Chat Work Mode all point in the same direction — agents-as-API-consumers becoming the default integration shape. If that holds through Q3, it changes the SaaS evaluation rubric.
