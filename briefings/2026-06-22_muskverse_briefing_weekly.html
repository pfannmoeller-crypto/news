SpaceX / xAI

IPO12 Jun 2026

SpaceX IPOs at a $1.75T valuation — the largest in history, xAI and X now inside the structure

SpaceX listed on Nasdaq under SPCX on 12 June, pricing at $135/share (~555.6M shares, ~$75B raised) at a $1.75T valuation and closing the first day +19% at $161 — a record debut. Crucially, the entity that went public already absorbed xAI in February 2026, so the listing bundles the launch business, Starlink, the Grok models, the X social network and xAI's data centers into one publicly traded conglomerate. That makes SPCX the cleanest public proxy for "the Musk AI-plus-space stack," and explains why it traded with the AI complex during Monday's mega-cap selloff rather than as a pure aerospace name.

OperationsMay–Jun 2026

Starship V3 flying, Starlink at ~9,800 sats and 10M+ subscribers — but growth is getting harder

The fundamentals behind the listing: Starship V3 launched successfully on 22 May — the first iteration built for operational missions (Artemis lunar support, eventual Mars). Starlink has scaled to ~9,800 operational satellites and 10M+ subscribers across 100 countries, and is now the revenue engine — 61% of 2025 sales, rising to ~69% in Q1 2026. The caveat CNBC flagged into the IPO: Starlink's growth is getting harder at this scale (subscriber acquisition cost, market saturation in early geographies), so the post-IPO story hinges on whether new geographies and direct-to-cell offset decelerating core growth.

Tesla

Q1 2026 · mixedQ1 print

Profit up, deliveries miss, capex tripled: Tesla is now an AI/robotaxi capex story

Tesla's Q1 framed the whole thesis: net income $477M (+17%) on revenue $22.39B (+16%), EPS $0.41 vs $0.39 expected — but it produced 408,386 vehicles and delivered only 358,023, a 50k+ gap that pushed inventory to 27 days (from 15). The pivot is explicit: 2026 capex guided above $25B, nearly triple the $9B spent in 2025, to fund AI compute and the robotaxi build-out. Robotaxi expanded to three Texas cities (Austin lead; Dallas, Houston live in April) with unsupervised FSD targeted in ~a dozen states by year-end — but Musk conceded robotaxi revenue won't be material in 2026, with real contribution pushed to 2027. The stock (~$432 on 1 June) sits below its late-2024 high.

DOGE

Wind-downsunset 4 Jul 2026

DOGE limps to its 4 July sunset — Musk long gone, functions absorbed, savings disputed

No material new Musk involvement here. Musk left the effort back in May 2025; by November 2025 OPM said DOGE no longer existed as a centralized entity, with functions absorbed into OPM and OMB. The charter that created it expires 4 July 2026, and while the website and ideas persist (embedded staffers, a "way of life" framing from the administration), the headline claim has aged badly: DOGE fell far short of the promised $2T in cuts, and even the ~$180B it claimed has been dogged by accounting errors and overstatements. For the Muskverse, this is a closed chapter — relevant mainly as reputational residue, not an active venture.

Legal

Musk v. Altmanverdict 18 May · appeal pending

Musk appeals the OpenAI loss he calls a "calendar technicality"

The headline legal item folds in from the now-closed OpenAI-trial routine. On 18 May an Oakland federal jury dismissed all of Musk's claims against OpenAI and Sam Altman — finding (after under two hours' deliberation) that the $150B suit was barred by a three-year statute of limitations, without reaching the merits of the "breach of charitable trust" argument. Musk called it a "calendar technicality" and vowed to appeal, so the feud continues into the appellate track. The timing is pointed: the appeal proceeds exactly as OpenAI files to go public (see AI briefing) — Musk's litigation is now a background risk factor hanging over the highest-profile IPO of the cycle, even if a procedural loss makes the appeal an uphill climb.

insightsEditor's view

The Muskverse just became a public-markets story — and the AI layer is the value

1. SPCX is the event that reframes everything. A $1.75T IPO with xAI, Starlink and X inside it makes SpaceX the public proxy for the entire Musk AI-plus-infrastructure stack. The day-one +19% says demand is there; the Monday slide says it now trades with the AI complex, not above it. The post-IPO test is Starlink's decelerating growth versus the AI/Grok optionality bundled in.

2. Tesla and SpaceX have swapped roles. SpaceX is the cash-generative, newly-public crown jewel; Tesla is the speculative capex story — tripling spend to $25B+ on robotaxi/AI while deliveries miss and the payoff slips to 2027. If you hold the Muskverse for AI exposure, the cleaner vehicle is now arguably SPCX, not TSLA.

3. The OpenAI feud has a money dimension now. Musk owns the leading non-OpenAI frontier-and-space conglomerate via SPCX and is simultaneously appealing his loss against OpenAI just as OpenAI files to IPO. Two trillion-dollar listings, two rival founders, one live lawsuit — the rivalry is now a public-markets event, not just a Silicon Valley grudge.

Cross-reference: SPCX and the OpenAI S-1 are the same liquidity window flagged in the AI and market briefings — the Monday mega-cap selloff that hit Alphabet/Amazon/Microsoft also caught the freshly-listed SpaceX. The OpenAI-IPO-timing market is a live Polymarket cluster (~51% by year-end).
