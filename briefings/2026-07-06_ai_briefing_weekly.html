Briefing summary

Washington enters the release cycle

Two frontier launches in ten days ran through the US government: OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (Sol/Terra/Luna) shipped as a limited preview coordinated with the White House under the 2 June AI Executive Order, and Anthropic's Fable 5 came back online worldwide on 1 July after Commerce lifted its 12 June export-control directive. Underneath, the compute layer is commoditising fast — Meta is building "Meta Compute" to rent out capacity, Microsoft stood up a $2.5B forward-deployed AI subsidiary, and Together AI doubled its valuation to $8.3B. Anthropic also shipped Claude Sonnet 5, the cheap agent workhorse of the summer, and China's Z.ai open-weight GLM-5.2 landed within a hair of Opus 4.8 on several benchmarks. Ten items sit at C-Score 4/5 or higher.

Claude Sonnet 5: near-Opus performance, agent-first, intro-priced
Anthropic's new midsize model lands close to Opus 4.8 on most work, with the pitch centred on agentic autonomy — planning, browser/terminal tool use, and finishing multi-step tasks where prior Sonnets stalled. Introductory pricing of $2/$10 per million tokens runs to 31 August, then $3/$15; it's the new default on Free and Pro plans and available across Max, Team and Enterprise.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Anthropic frames Sonnet-class models as the birthplace of the agentic era — 3.5, 3.6 and 3.7 were the first Claudes to show strong coding and tool use. But over the past year the sharpest agentic gains had migrated up to the Opus tier, leaving Sonnet as the value pick rather than the frontier. Sonnet 5's explicit job is to close that gap: it succeeds Sonnet 4.6 and is positioned as "the most agentic Sonnet yet."

Die Details

On the cost-performance curves Anthropic published for BrowseComp (agentic search) and OSWorld-Verified (computer use), Sonnet 5 is a strict improvement over 4.6 and, at high/xhigh effort, can match Opus 4.8 on some tasks — while spanning a far wider cost range. Opus 4.8 is priced $5/$25; Sonnet 5 sits at $3/$15 standard, $2/$10 through 31 Aug. A caveat buried in the footnotes: Sonnet 5 uses an updated tokenizer (like Opus 4.7) that maps the same text to roughly 1.0–1.35× more tokens, so the intro pricing is calibrated to make the switch "roughly cost-neutral." Anthropic also raised rate limits across Chat, Cowork, Claude Code and the Platform. It ships with the real-time cyber safeguards used on Opus 4.7/4.8 (lighter than Fable 5's), and its safety audit showed lower hallucination, sycophancy and misaligned-behaviour rates than 4.6 — though still higher than Opus 4.8 and Mythos Preview.

Einordnung

This is the commoditisation of "good enough agentic intelligence." Named early-access partners — Cursor (Sualeh Asif), Lovable, Kiro (AWS), ClickHouse, Box, Eve — all stress the same thing: the model finishes work end-to-end and checks itself without prompting. That reliability at $2/$10 is what makes Sonnet 5 the default coding/agent workhorse for the summer, and it directly pressures OpenAI's mid-tier (Terra at $2.50/$15) and Google's Gemini 3.5 Flash on the same price/quality axis. Watch the tokenizer point: real bills may run above the headline rate depending on content type.

Watch-Items

The 1 September price step-up from $2/$10 to $3/$15 — re-benchmark cost-per-task before then. Whether independent evals (Willison, Artificial Analysis) confirm the "matches Opus 4.8" claim outside Anthropic's chosen benchmarks. And how the tokenizer change lands on real-world token counts for German-language and structured-data workloads.

OPENAI26 Jun
GPT-5.6 launches as Sol, Terra and Luna — under government coordination
OpenAI split its new generation into three tiers — Sol (flagship, $5/$30 per 1M tokens), Terra (balanced, $2.50/$15) and Luna (fast, $1/$6) — but the launch itself is the story: initial access is a limited preview for a small group of "trusted partners" whose participation was shared with the US government, following the 2 June executive order. General availability is promised "in the coming weeks." First US frontier launch to run through an explicit government gate.
▶ Mehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

The new naming system decouples generation (the number, 5.6) from capability tier (Sol/Terra/Luna as durable names that can advance on their own cadence). OpenAI says it previewed the models and their capabilities to the US government ahead of launch "as part of our ongoing engagement," and at the government's request is starting with a limited preview before releasing broadly. OpenAI is unusually blunt that it dislikes this: "We don't believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default. It keeps the best tools from users, developers, enterprises, cyber defenders, and global partners who need them."

Die Details

Sol introduces a new max reasoning effort and an ultra mode that spins up subagents for complex work. Claimed results: state-of-the-art on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (command-line workflows), stronger GeneBench v1 (genomics) than GPT-5.5 using fewer tokens, and on ExploitBench it is "competitive with Mythos Preview using only ~1/3 of the output tokens." Crucially, OpenAI states Sol does not cross the "Cyber Critical" threshold in its Preparedness Framework — in Chromium/Firefox tests it found bugs and exploitation primitives but did not autonomously produce a full-chain exploit. Terra is pitched as GPT-5.5-competitive at 2× cheaper; Luna as strong capability at lowest cost. OpenAI dedicated 700,000+ A100-equivalent GPU hours to automated red-teaming for universal jailbreaks, and adds prompt-caching changes (30-min minimum cache life, 1.25× cache-write billing). Sol also arrives on Cerebras at up to 750 tokens/sec in July for select customers.

Einordnung

The gate is the second-order story. Read alongside Fable 5's export-control episode, model availability is now a live policy variable in the US — the government is inserting itself between "model finished" and "model shipped" for frontier releases with material cyber capability. OpenAI is publicly cooperating while lobbying to make the process "repeatable" and short-lived via the cyber Executive Order framework. For buyers, the practical takeaway: the flagship you benchmark today may not be generally callable for weeks, and access can be conditioned.

Watch-Items

When (and whether) GA actually lands "in the coming weeks," and how wide the preview partner list is. Whether the government-access step recurs on the next release or is codified into a lighter, faster process. And the expanded eval suite OpenAI promised at GA — the preview system card is deliberately partial.

ANTHROPIC1 Jul
Fable 5 returns worldwide after Commerce lifts export controls
The US Department of Commerce lifted its 12 June export-control directive on 30 June, and Anthropic began redeploying Claude Fable 5 globally on 1 July across Claude.ai, the Platform, Claude Code and Cowork. Reported condition: a new safety classifier blocking the specific jailbreak at issue in over 99% of attempts. Together with the GPT-5.6 gate, this makes model availability itself a policy variable — a first.
▶ Mehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Anthropic released Fable 5 and Mythos 5 (same underlying model, different safeguards) on 9 June. On 12 June the government applied export controls after Amazon researchers reported a method of bypassing Fable 5's safeguards — prompting it to identify software vulnerabilities and, in one case, produce code demonstrating exploitation. Because the order took effect immediately and Anthropic couldn't verify nationality in real time, it suspended access to both models for all users. The controls were lifted 30 June; Mythos 5 access was restored to a set of US Glasswing organisations following approval on 26 June.

Die Details

Anthropic's own testing undercut the severity claim: less capable models — Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5, Kimi K2.7 — could identify the same vulnerabilities, and every model tested (down to Haiku 4.5 and Sonnet 4.6) could produce the same single exploit demonstration. The behaviour was a borderline case for Fable 5's deliberately oversized "safety margin," which blocks many benign requests out of caution. The fix: a trained classifier that blocks the reported technique in >99% of cases and reroutes blocked requests to Opus 4.8; CAISI (Commerce's Center for AI Standards and Innovation) tested and endorsed both old and new safeguards. Rollout terms: Fable 5 included for up to 50% of weekly usage limits through 7 July for Pro/Max/Team/select Enterprise, then via usage credits; AWS, Google Cloud and Microsoft Foundry re-enabled "as quickly as possible."

Einordnung

The bigger output is institutional: Anthropic, with Amazon, Microsoft, Google and other Glasswing partners, is drafting a consensus industry framework to score jailbreak severity on four axes (capability gain, breadth, ease of weaponization, discoverability) — modelled on CVSS for software vulns. It's also committing to pre-release government access for national-security-relevant models, rapid safeguard information-sharing, and joint research/compute for government testing. In other words, the 19-day outage is being turned into a template for how frontier releases get vetted. For any workflow that depended solely on Fable 5, this was a real, unplanned 19-day availability gap.

Watch-Items

Whether the four-axis jailbreak framework gets adopted beyond the Glasswing circle. False-positive rate on the new classifier during routine coding (Anthropic admits it flags more benign requests). And whether the "pre-release government access" commitment hardens into durable, transparent regulation applied equally across labs — or stays ad hoc.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte

GLM-5.2 is the latest in a run of cheap, near-frontier Chinese open-weight models — the same pattern set by DeepSeek V4 and Kimi K2.6 earlier this year. Z.ai (formerly Zhipu) released it 16 June under an unrestricted MIT license, positioning open weights and low token cost as the wedge against closed US frontier models.

Die Details

Einordnung

The strategic point isn't any single benchmark — it's that an MIT-licensed model you can self-host now sits within a point or two of the closed US frontier on agentic coding, at a sixth of the cost. That is exactly the dynamic feeding the Together AI thesis (open-weight inference on neoclouds tripling year-on-year) and the pricing pressure on Sonnet 5 and Terra. For a European operator, open-weight near-frontier models are the hedge against both US export politics and closed-model pricing — though data-governance and provenance questions around Chinese weights remain live.

Watch-Items

Whether Western enterprises actually deploy Chinese open weights at scale given security/provenance scrutiny. And any US policy response to open-weight Chinese models nearing frontier coding capability.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte

The move directly answers the most-cited stat in enterprise AI: MIT's Project NANDA found ~95% of enterprise generative-AI pilots deliver zero measurable P&L impact. Die Details

"Forward-deployed engineering" means Microsoft sends its own technical staff to work inside a customer's operations to design, build, deploy and run AI systems on-site — the model Palantir pioneered. Scale: ~6,000 people, $2.5B commitment. Coverage (CNBC, TechCrunch, GeekWire) frames it explicitly as targeting the pilot-failure problem, turning Azure AI capacity into governed, working enterprise systems rather than shelfware.

Einordnung

This is a structural signal that the industry has hit the "last mile" wall: the constraint on AI value is no longer model capability but integration, change management and governance inside messy enterprises. It also reprices the consulting/SI market — Microsoft is inserting itself where Accenture, Deloitte and Palantir operate, using its model + cloud + now bodies. For a mid-sized operator, the useful read is validation of the thesis that AI ROI comes from deployment discipline, not tool procurement — and a reminder that the vendors themselves now concede demos don't equal results.

Watch-Items

The 10× token-cost claim, if it holds even partially in production, is the mechanical engine behind falling inference prices — the same trend that makes Sonnet 5 at $2/$10 and GLM-5.2 at 1/6th cost sustainable. DEV TOOLING28 Jul RC
MCP goes stateless: the 2026-07-28 spec release candidate lands
The next Model Context Protocol revision makes requests self-contained (carrying protocol version, client info and capabilities), so any server instance can handle any call — the change that makes MCP deployable like a normal horizontally-scaled web service. Meaningful for anyone running MCP servers in production. Final spec expected 28 July 2026.
▶ Mehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

MCP (now stewarded under the Agentic AI Foundation / Linux Foundation) started as a practical way to connect AI apps to tools and data. Prior versions used Streamable HTTP with a server-issued Mcp-Session-Id that clients carried across requests — forcing sticky sessions, shared session stores and MCP-aware gateways. Manageable in experiments, painful behind load balancers and rate limiters in production.

Die Details

The 2026-07-28 release candidate makes MCP stateless at the protocol layer: a tools/call can be self-contained, with protocol version, client info and capabilities travelling with the request, and headers (Mcp-Method, Mcp-Name) letting infrastructure route without parsing the body. Application state doesn't vanish — it becomes explicit via visible handles (e.g. a server returns a basket_id the model passes back), which improves agent reasoning and observability. Other changes: Extensions get reverse-DNS identifiers, ext-* repos and independent versioning (first examples: MCP Apps for sandboxed HTML UIs, and Tasks for long-running work via tasks/get, tasks/update, tasks/cancel); Roots, Sampling and Logging are deprecated (annotation-only, ≥12 months to removal) in favour of tool params/resource URIs, direct LLM-provider APIs, and stderr/OpenTelemetry respectively; full JSON Schema 2020-12 support for tools; and tightened OAuth/OIDC rules for one-client-many-servers deployments. There are breaking changes — a 10-week window (RC now, final 28 July) is for SDK/client maintainers to validate.

Einordnung

This is MCP "growing up" from clever demo to production infrastructure: statelessness makes MCP servers scale and debug like ordinary web services, and the governance additions (feature lifecycle, conformance-tested SEPs, neutral stewardship) reduce lock-in to any single vendor's implementation. For anyone building agentic systems on MCP — increasingly the default plumbing across Anthropic, OpenAI, Google and xAI tooling — this is the release to test against before it hardens. AAIF's own advice: find hidden session dependencies now.

Watch-Items

Whether the 28 July final ships on schedule. SDK/client adoption speed given breaking changes. And whether pushing model access out to provider APIs (deprecating Sampling) hurts cross-provider portability, as the RC discussion flags.

▶ Mehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

The AI Act Omnibus is part of the EU's broader push to simplify digital regulation. It doesn't change the Act's risk-based architecture, but it clarifies requirements, extends deadlines, and adds targeted prohibitions — largely because the Commission hadn't yet issued the harmonised standards and guidance that high-risk operators need to comply on the original timeline.

Die Details

Concrete deadline changes (per Latham & Watkins): stand-alone high-risk AI system (HRAIS) obligations move from 2 Aug 2026 to 2 Dec 2027; HRAIS that are regulated products/safety components move to 2 Aug 2028; systems placed on the market before those dates escape HRAIS rules unless "substantially modified." A grandfathering rule gives pre-existing generative-AI systems until 2 Dec 2026 to meet watermarking/labeling rules (Art. 50 transparency obligations still apply from 2 Aug 2026 for new systems). New from 2 Dec 2026: a prohibition on "nudifier" apps generating non-consensual intimate imagery/CSAM, with fines up to €35M or 7% of worldwide turnover. Scope relief: an industrial-AI carveout (machinery already regulated elsewhere), a narrower "safety component" definition, and — critically for mid-caps — the SME simplified-compliance framework extended to companies up to 750 employees and €150M revenue (simplified guidance, reduced fines, sandbox access, standardised templates). Bias-detection using GDPR special-category data is also made easier.

Einordnung

For Stefan's pet-food companies (AlphaPet, SmartPetPro): the 750-employee / €150M mid-cap relief would cover them if any AI system they use ever falls into HRAIS scope — but note the everyday tools (customer-service chatbots, marketing content, the xAI voice agents above) are generally not high-risk; HRAIS covers recruitment, credit scoring, biometrics, critical infrastructure and regulated safety components. The transparency obligations (disclose AI chatbots, label AI-generated content) are the ones most likely to touch a commerce operator, and those still bite from Aug/Dec 2026. Practical stance: no HRAIS scramble, but do prepare chatbot-disclosure and AI-content-labeling before the Aug/Dec 2026 dates.
