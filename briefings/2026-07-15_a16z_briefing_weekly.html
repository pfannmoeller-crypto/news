LEAD DEALDATA CENTERS01–02 Jul
a16z leads Switch's ~$2B round with a ~$400M cheque at a near-$50B valuation
a16z is leading a roughly $2 billion financing for Las Vegas–based data-center operator Switch Inc., committing about $400 million itself, according to Bloomberg reporting confirmed through the week by Benzinga and Cryptobriefing. The round values Switch at nearly $50 billion including debt (roughly $19 billion of equity value before the new money) — more than four times the $11 billion DigitalBridge and IFM Investors paid to take it private in 2022. Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan are arranging the financing, which could precede an IPO as soon as 2027. This is a16z's most literal expression yet of its "AI needs physical infrastructure" thesis.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Switch runs hyperscale data-center campuses across Nevada, Michigan, Texas and Georgia, serving clients that reportedly include Nvidia, Google, Tesla and Logitech. It was taken private in 2022 by DigitalBridge Group and IFM Investors for about $11 billion including debt; DigitalBridge itself has since agreed to be acquired by SoftBank Group, so a16z is buying into an asset sitting under a shifting ownership stack. The cheque follows a16z's January 2026 $15B raise, which explicitly flagged infrastructure and "American Dynamism" as priority buckets — Switch is the marquee deployment of that mandate. It also rhymes with the firm's late-June Netris Series A: both bets say the constraint on AI is now power, cooling and network fabric, not model weights.

Die Details

Publicly reported terms: round size ~$2 billion; a16z lead commitment ~$400 million; blended valuation "nearly $50 billion including debt," with equity value cited at roughly $19 billion pre-money; Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan as arrangers. Co-investors beyond a16z were not disclosed in the reporting reviewed. Note the debt-inclusive headline number — the equity a16z is pricing against is materially smaller than the $50B figure, and the exact instrument (primary equity vs. structured/preferred) is not public. Treat the valuation as directional until a definitive filing or company statement lands.

Einordnung

For a firm built on software ("software is eating the world"), leading a capital-intensive, real-estate-heavy data-center round is a deliberate identity stretch — and a signal that a16z believes the AI value chain's scarcest, most defensible layer is now compute real estate, not apps. It plants a16z alongside the Blackstone/Apollo/BlackRock crowd financing the data-center "supercycle," a different competitive set than Sequoia or Benchmark. If the 2027 IPO lands, Switch becomes a visible growth-fund mark; if AI capex expectations wobble, a16z is exposed to the most cyclical, leverage-sensitive part of the stack.

Viking Global Investors, AlleyCorp and Ulysses Capital joined the equity. Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Pearl Health sits in value-based care: it gives independent primary-care practices the software and risk tooling to take on Medicare accountable-care arrangements (getting paid to keep patients healthy rather than per-visit). CEO Michael Kopko frames the mission as "healthcare should reward keeping people healthy, not just treating them when they are sick." The company reached profitability in 2025 and is projecting roughly $500 million in gross savings and a tripling of its patient base by end-2026 — the kind of traction that lets a16z lead an equity round in a business that is already self-sustaining rather than a pre-revenue bet.

Die Details

Total announced: $110 million — split into ~$50 million equity (a16z leading; Viking Global, AlleyCorp, Ulysses Capital participating) and a ~$60 million credit facility from Trinity Capital. The round was not explicitly labeled Series C in the primary release, and a post-money valuation was not disclosed. Use of proceeds: expand AI capabilities, accelerate enterprise health-system and payer partnerships, push further into Medicare Advantage, and build new risk-management products. Scale metrics cited: 10,000+ providers, 40+ states, ~$3.6B annualized medical spend under management.

Einordnung

This is a16z's Bio + Health franchise doing what it does best: backing "AI wrapped around a regulated payment model" rather than a raw model lab. The interesting tell is the debt-plus-equity structure on a profitable company — a16z is buying growth-stage ownership while Trinity's facility funds working capital, minimizing dilution. It also fits the broader 2026 pattern of investors preferring AI plays with a clear, defensible revenue engine (here, Medicare economics) over speculative frontier bets. For a16z healthcare, a profitable, scaled asset is a de-risked flagship it can point IPO-track founders toward.

MARC ANDREESSENFEDERAL RESERVE09 Jul
Marc Andreessen is named to co-lead a new Federal Reserve "Productivity and Jobs" task force
On 9 July, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh named a16z co-founder Marc Andreessen to co-lead a newly created "Productivity and Jobs" task force charged with assessing how general-purpose technologies — AI in particular — affect economic output and employment. The panel feeds research and feedback to the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC), which sets US interest rates, though it does not set rates itself. Andreessen serves alongside Stanford economist Charles I. Jones (currently on leave working at Anthropic) and Microsoft EVP / Xbox CEO Asha Sharma. Preliminary findings are expected in the fall.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Warsh, who took the Fed chair in 2026, created the task force as part of a broader review of how the central bank makes monetary-policy decisions. The core question is genuinely unsettled inside the Fed: does AI ultimately lower inflation via productivity gains, or raise it via the massive infrastructure spending needed to build it out? Andreessen — a relentless AI accelerationist who has argued "any deceleration of AI will cost lives" and that AI is "already a better doctor than 99.99% of human doctors" — is now positioned to shape how the Fed reads that trade-off. It is a striking escalation of a16z's Washington footprint, following the firm's American Dynamism push and the founders' public alignment with the current administration.

Die Details

Task force: "Productivity and Jobs," one of several Warsh stood up. Mandate: assess how general-purpose technologies (including AI) affect output and employment; provide research and feedback to the FOMC. Members: Andreessen (co-lead), Charles I. Jones (Stanford, on leave at Anthropic), Asha Sharma (Microsoft EVP, Xbox CEO). It operates independently with Fed staff support and does not set interest rates. Announced 9 July 2026; work to begin within weeks; preliminary findings expected fall 2026. Warsh's framing: to "ensure the Fed is best positioned to achieve our objectives."
