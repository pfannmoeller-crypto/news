Tesla

Tesla Apr 29 2026

Tesla Semi rolls off high-volume production line — nine years after reveal

The first Tesla Semi rolled off the high-volume production line at Tesla's Sparks, Nevada facility on April 29, marking the transition from the pilot line that produced only a few hundred units since October 2023. The 5.4-million-square-foot factory is designed for annual capacity of 50,000 trucks, though 2026 output is expected in the 5,000–15,000 range as the ramp proceeds. Two variants ship: a 325-mile Standard Range and a 500-mile Long Range, both with tri-motor powertrains and 4680 battery cells manufactured on-site. Tesla simultaneously launched "Semi Charging for Business," a commercial charging network play. (Electrek, CleanTechnica)

So what: The Semi is Tesla's first new mass-production vehicle line since Model Y. It diversifies revenue away from the consumer-EV business that contracted in 2025 and positions Tesla in commercial fleet electrification — a segment where BYD has less foothold.

Tesla May 1 2026

$573M in cross-entity transactions revealed; TSLA at ~$391; FSD clears Dutch regulator

Tesla's amended 10-K filing disclosed $573 million in 2025 revenue from SpaceX and xAI, making the financial entanglement between Musk entities concrete and public for the first time. (Electrek, Motley Fool)

So what: The $573M disclosure is the first hard number on cross-entity revenue flow. EU FSD approval would be a significant regulatory unlock for the robotaxi narrative in Europe. The capex bump from $20B to $25B+ reflects Cybercab, Semi, and Optimus ramp spending.

xAI Late Apr 2026

Grok 4.3 launches with always-on reasoning, 1M-token context, and aggressive pricing

xAI released Grok 4.3, featuring always-on reasoning (no toggle needed) and a 1-million-token context window. API pricing is set at $1.25 / $2.50 per million input/output tokens (up to 200K input), roughly halving the cost of its predecessor Grok 4.2 ($2/$6). Alongside the model, xAI shipped Custom Voices, a voice-cloning suite, signaling a push into personalized audio AI. The pricing undercuts OpenAI and Anthropic on most comparable tiers. (VentureBeat, tbreak)

So what: Grok 4.3's pricing is a race-to-the-bottom move enabled by Colossus scale economics. Always-on reasoning closes the UX gap with OpenAI's o-series. The voice-cloning suite is a play for consumer lock-in through personalization — expect regulatory attention in the EU under the AI Act (KI-Verordnung).

SpaceX

1 card
SpaceX May 12 target

Starship Flight 12 — V3 maiden flight targeting May 12; 50th Falcon launch of 2026 completed

SpaceX confirmed Starship Flight 12 for May 12 (22:30 UTC window), the maiden flight of the Version 3 system — a clean-sheet redesign. V3 pairs Booster 19 and Ship 39, both powered by Raptor 3 engines, stands 408 feet tall, and is designed to carry over 100 tons to LEO (Low Earth Orbit), nearly triple V2's ~35-ton capacity. The mission is suborbital: both booster and ship target splashdown rather than a tower catch, deliberately stepping back in ambition to validate the new architecture. This will also be the first launch from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase. Separately, SpaceX completed its 50th Falcon launch of 2026, maintaining a pace that competitors cannot match. (Basenor, New Space Economy)

So what: V3 at 100+ tons to LEO is a generational leap — it turns Starship from a development program into a commercially viable heavy-lift system. Success on May 12 would de-risk the IPO narrative substantially. The deliberate splashdown-only profile for a maiden V3 flight shows SpaceX learning from Starship V2's incremental approach.

Politics Apr 28 – May 4 2026

DOGE lawsuits advance — judge denies dismissal; Musk unavailable for deposition; July 4 sunset looms

A federal judge denied the Trump administration's bid to drop a lawsuit against Musk for his former advisory role in DOGE (Department of Government Efficiency), ruling that the case can proceed. Separately, CNN and the Washington Post reported that Musk's legal adversaries cannot locate him to serve deposition questions about his actions inside DOGE during its January–May 2025 operational period. New disclosures revealed how DOGE actually operated, including ChatGPT usage inside government workflows. The entity's formal sunset date remains July 4, 2026, per Trump's directive, though many DOGE staffers and ideas are now permanently embedded across federal agencies. A CBS News analysis found that claimed $160B in DOGE savings have been offset by $135B in downstream costs. (The Hill, CNN, Washington Post)

So what: The DOGE lawsuits represent a slow-burn legal risk that could force Musk into sworn testimony about government decision-making — separate from and potentially more politically damaging than the OpenAI trial. The $160B savings vs. $135B costs framing undermines the efficiency narrative.

OpenAI Trial · Musk v. Altman

2 cards
Trial Apr 28–May 2 · Week 1

Musk testifies for three days: "I came up with the idea, the name, recruited the key people"

The trial opened April 28 in Oakland before Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers with a nine-person jury. Musk testified over three days (Tuesday through Thursday), dominating Week 1. Core claim: Musk says his ~$38M in donations to OpenAI was used for unauthorized commercial purposes, and that Microsoft's $10B investment was the tipping point that violated the nonprofit mission. Musk told the jury: "You can't just steal a charity." He acknowledged he is not entirely against a for-profit unit, but said it became "the tail wagging the dog," accusing Altman and Brockman of self-enrichment. The trial is split into two phases: a liability phase (expected to conclude by May 21) and a remedies phase if wrongdoing is found. Altman and Brockman are expected to testify in Week 2–3. (CNBC, Washington Post)

So what: The trial is now the highest-visibility legal proceeding in AI history. The liability phase outcome by May 21 could either validate Musk's claim that OpenAI's for-profit conversion was unauthorized — creating regulatory precedent for nonprofit-to-commercial AI transitions — or undermine Musk's credibility as an AI-governance voice.

Trial Apr 30 2026 · cross-examination

Musk admits xAI distilled OpenAI models — calls it industry-standard practice

Under cross-examination by OpenAI's attorneys, Musk was asked whether xAI has used distillation techniques on OpenAI models to train Grok. He confirmed it, asserting it is a "general practice among AI companies." This admission is significant because it simultaneously (a) validates OpenAI's claim that its models have proprietary value worth protecting, (b) undercuts Musk's argument that OpenAI's technology should have been freely shared, and (c) creates a potential counter-claim surface for OpenAI regarding xAI's use of their outputs. MIT Technology Review summarized Week 1 as: "Musk says he was duped, warns AI could kill us all, and admits that xAI distills OpenAI's models." (TechCrunch, MIT Tech Review)

So what: The distillation admission is the single most consequential moment of Week 1. It gives OpenAI's lawyers a narrative weapon ("he's suing us while copying our work") and may inform the remedies phase. For xAI, the commercial risk is manageable — distillation is indeed widespread — but the reputational and legal risk during a live trial is acute.

Other Ventures · Neuralink & Boring Company

2 cards
Neuralink 2026 roadmap

Neuralink plans high-volume BCI production and automated surgery in 2026

Musk announced that Neuralink will begin high-volume production of BCI (Brain-Computer Interface) devices in 2026, moving to a "streamlined, almost entirely automated surgical procedure." A key technical advance: device threads will now go through the dura mater (Hirnhaut) without needing to remove it — reducing surgical invasiveness. Twelve people with severe paralysis are currently using the implants to control digital and physical tools through thought alone. The Blindsight implant for vision restoration is scheduled for its first patient trial in 2026. A former FDA (Food and Drug Administration) executive, David McMullen, joined as head of medical affairs in December 2025, bridging clinical, research, and regulatory efforts. (MDDIonline, Fierce Biotech)

So what: Moving from individual implants to high-volume production is the step from clinical trial to commercial medical device. Automated surgery is essential for scaling beyond specialized neurosurgery centers. The Blindsight trial — if successful — would be the most publicly visible demonstration of BCI utility to date.

Boring Co Expanding footprint 2026

Boring Company expanding to Nashville, Dallas, New Orleans, Dubai — but facing local resistance

The Boring Company is in its most active expansion phase ever, with projects in Nashville (Music City Loop — two 10-mile tunnels to the airport, shaft dug, machine assembled), Dallas (mile-long tunnel study won via company contest), New Orleans (assessment phase), Orlando (Universal theme park connector), and Dubai (construction started Feb 2026, launch expected 2027). However, Nashville's Metro City Council voted to formally oppose the Music City Loop in March, citing poor track record on environmental and workplace safety. Tennessee Republican lawmakers subsequently passed legislation insulating the project from local officials. Las Vegas operations remain the core: 8 stations, 5+ miles of tunnel, 68 miles approved. (CNBC Nashville, Texas Tribune)

So what: Boring Company is the lowest-strategic-value Muskverse entity but it is quietly building a real pipeline of municipal projects. The Nashville pattern — local opposition overridden by state legislation — will likely repeat and creates political friction. Dubai is the first international project and a proof-of-concept for non-US expansion.

edit_note Editor's View

Six takeaways from the week of May 4, 2026

  • 1
    The distillation admission reshapes the trial. Musk confirming that xAI trained Grok on OpenAI outputs is the most strategically significant disclosure of the week. It is honest, probably legally defensible as industry-standard, but it hands OpenAI's lawyers a narrative that undermines the moral framing of the entire lawsuit. Watch how this plays in Week 2 when Altman takes the stand.
  • 2
    Tesla is quietly becoming a multi-vehicle manufacturer again. Semi production start, Cybercab pilot, Optimus on deck for summer. The car business that contracted 3% in 2025 is being surrounded by new product lines. Whether these lines deliver revenue at scale before the valuation thesis requires it is the open question — but the operational execution is real.
  • 3
    Starship V3 on May 12 is the week's most important upcoming event. A successful maiden flight of a 100-ton-to-LEO vehicle would be the most significant launch milestone since Falcon 9 first landed. It directly de-risks the SpaceX IPO narrative. A failure would not be catastrophic (iterative testing is expected) but would shift the IPO timeline conversation.
  • 4
    The $573M cross-entity revenue disclosure makes the Muskverse's financial interconnections concrete. SpaceX and xAI paying Tesla over half a billion dollars in 2025 is not a conflict-of-interest theory anymore — it is an audited financial fact. This will feature in future shareholder litigation, SEC scrutiny, and governance debates around the IPO.
  • 5
    Grok's distribution strategy is expanding beyond the Musk ecosystem. CarPlay integration means Grok is no longer confined to X and Tesla cabins. The pricing aggression on Grok 4.3 ($1.25/M input tokens) is a market-share play that only works if Colossus's scale advantages are real. If they are, xAI could be the price leader in frontier AI within 12 months.
  • 6
    Musk is in two courtrooms simultaneously. The OpenAI trial in Oakland and the DOGE lawsuit progressing through federal court create a dual legal exposure that constrains his bandwidth and creates deposition risk. The DOGE case — where he cannot be located for service — is the slower-moving but potentially more personally damaging proceeding.
