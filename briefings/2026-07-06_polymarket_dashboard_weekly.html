The substance is good; the process wobbled. Four weeks, three regimes. In the June 12 edition the cluster was deteriorating: the April ceasefire had broken (strikes June 10–11), the deal-by-June-30 leg whipsawed paradoxically up to ~31%, and every near-dated Iran price carried a 3/5 flag. The June 15–18 memorandum flipped the regime: the permanent-peace-by-December leg, which had traded ~74% before the strikes, re-set at ~68.5% on criteria-strictness (the crowd read the MOU as a framework that might not satisfy “permanent”) while deal-by-June-30 collapsed to low single digits and duly resolved No. The third regime arrived in late June: the ~$345M complex went to dispute, then resolved YES via the UMA vote — so the price series ends not in a quote but in a contested legal fact. The tradeable expression of the cluster migrated to oil (Brent ~$92 pre-deal → ~$83 June 22 → ~$72.20 now, a −22% round trip of the entire war premium) and to successor markets: “Next round of US–Iran peace talks by…?” launched June 23 and has already drawn ~$4.9M in volume — the fastest-growing geopolitical book on the board. [Polymarket: next round of talks]

Die Signale im Detail

  • Permanent peace deal (~$345M, resolved YES): the resolution rules required an agreement that “explicitly indicates that military hostilities between the United States and Iran have ended or will permanently cease” — temporary ceasefires explicitly excluded. The dispute turned on whether a memorandum with a built-in 60-day transitional phase can be “permanent.” UMA's process: token holders debate evidence in a Discord channel, then vote over 48–72 hours, voting power proportional to staked tokens. Bloomberg's analysis found nine wallets control more than half of all UMA voting tokens; in this vote the largest whale held ~16.7%, the top five ~41.7%, the top seven ~50.3% — all reportedly voting YES. Losing bettors are threatening a lawsuit and formally challenging the mechanism. [TNW] [Ynet: lawsuit threat] [Calcalist]
  • Next-round-of-talks market (~$4.9M): Qatar's Foreign Ministry says the next senior meeting will be scheduled “at the earliest possible time” after the funeral (July 4–9); reporting points to a resumption around July 11 in Islamabad. Crucially, technical-level sessions continued through the pause — enrichment limits, sanctions relief, Hormuz access — and Iran has agreed to host IAEA (International Atomic Energy Agency) inspectors. The senior track paused; the working track did not.
  • Ceasefire-continues / regime-stability legs: live but without corroborated prints this window (2/5). A separate “Iranian regime falls before 2027” market exists as the succession-tail instrument — worth a corroborated read next edition given Mojtaba's absence.
  • Oil as the honest shadow market: the July 1 dip on Iran skipping the Qatar round is the tell — crude read the skip as less deal-friction premium, not more war risk. Oil currently trusts the de-escalation more than the resolution-disputed Polymarket complex did.

Szenarien

(a) Islamabad happens ~July 11 (base case): the senior track resumes with ~5 weeks left on the clock; final-deal-by-mid-August legs firm, the cluster returns to improving, Brent stays low or grinds lower. (b) Drift: the funeral ends but no senior round convenes within a week — each idle week consumes ~15% of the remaining window; the likely outcome becomes a deadline extension, which strict final-deal markets price as failure even while ceasefire-continues legs stay high. The cluster bifurcates: peace holds, “deal” misses. (c) Succession shock: evidence Mojtaba cannot govern — hardline consolidation, IRGC (Revolutionary Guard) assertion — re-prices Hormuz risk, oil, and every leg in the same hour. This is the tail the current calm is underpricing.

Watch-Items

  • July 11 Islamabad round: happens / slips / silently dies.
  • Mojtaba Khamenei: any verified public appearance; continued written-only communication is itself data.
  • IAEA inspectors physically entering Iran — the first hard compliance fact of the MOU.
  • The UMA challenge and Bloomberg's insider-trading reporting — any Polymarket rule change hits every long-dated price on this board.
