This week the feud flared alongside the litigation: after Apple sued OpenAI, Musk posted "Scam Altman strikes again," Altman fired back that Musk is misleading investors on "short-term space datacenters," and the two traded blows as Grok 4.5 and GPT-5.6 "Sol" launched days apart. Separately, xAI is appealing the dismissal of its trade-secret suit against OpenAI.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte — Musk filed in August 2024 alleging OpenAI and Altman betrayed the founding non-profit charter by converting toward a for-profit structure — claims of breach of charitable trust, fraud and unjust enrichment. Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers trimmed some claims but let fraud and unjust enrichment reach a jury; she also ruled "existential risk" out of scope. Musk testified 28–30 April 2026. In an unusual move, he had amended his request so any monetary damages would go to OpenAI's non-profit charity, and he sought Altman's removal from the board. OpenAI counter-sued in April 2025, accusing Musk of bad-faith tactics to benefit his own competing interests (xAI).

Die Details — The May 2026 jury never weighed the merits: it found the three-year statutory window for the charitable-trust and unjust-enrichment claims had expired because Musk knew of OpenAI's commercial pivot by ~2021. Deliberation took under two hours. No damages were awarded; a Musk win could have redirected up to ~$150B to the original charitable foundation. His appeal turns on a single legal question — when the limitations clock started: Musk's team argues it should run from OpenAI's finalized 2023–24 restructuring, not from his earlier general awareness. That is a genuine, appealable question of law (accrual date), which is why "technicality" cuts both ways — it lost him the trial but is also the cleanest possible basis for a reversal-and-remand.

Einordnung — The legal case and the commercial war are now fully intertwined. This week's Fortune and Bloomberg reporting shows the rivalry operating on three fronts at once: the courtroom (this appeal, plus xAI's separate trade-secret appeal), the product race (Grok 4.5 vs GPT-5.6 "Sol," launched days apart, with Altman claiming benchmark superiority), and the daily X brawl ("Scam Altman" / "obsessed with me again"). Apple's fresh lawsuit against OpenAI gave Musk a new cudgel. For the portfolio, the appeal is a low-probability, high-payoff option — a Ninth Circuit reversal would revive an existential-scale claim against xAI's chief competitor — but it will take quarters to resolve, and the near-term impact is reputational and strategic rather than financial.

Watch-Items — (1) formal notice-of-appeal filing and the Ninth Circuit briefing schedule; (2) the accrual-date legal question — the entire appeal hinges on it; (3) the parallel xAI trade-secret appeal; (4) the Apple-vs-OpenAI suit and whether xAI aligns with Apple; (5) whether the GPT-5.6-vs-Grok product race spills into further legal filings.
