Week of 8 May 2026 · covering 1–8 May
 
Briefing summary
Anthropic's recovery week: shut out of the Pentagon, then strikes three industry-defining partnerships in five days.
A week ago the headline was "Anthropic blacklisted from DoD." Since then: $200B Google TPU commitment, all of SpaceX's Colossus 1 (300 MW, 220K GPUs), and a $1.5B+ enterprise services JV with Goldman, Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, GIC, Apollo, and Sequoia. OpenAI launched a parallel services JV the same day. Trump's CAISI brought Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI into the same pre-launch national-security testing framework Anthropic and OpenAI already signed in 2024. The compute, distribution, and oversight maps all redrew this week.
 
Anthropic's compute & distribution build · 4 stories
Anthropic + Google Reported 5–6 May 2026
 
Anthropic commits $200B to Google Cloud over five years — TPU capacity locked through end of decade.
 
The Information first reported the commitment, sized at roughly $200B over five years, beginning in 2027 — extending the April expansion that secured access to up to one million TPUs and over one gigawatt of capacity coming online during 2026. The contract reportedly accounts for more than 40% of Google's disclosed revenue backlog. Google and Anthropic declined to confirm; Reuters could not independently verify. Even at face value with one source, the directional read is clear: Anthropic is locking in the largest single TPU commitment ever disclosed, and Google has secured its anchor customer for the multi-gigawatt buildout.
Read more: The Information (paywall) · Engadget.

Anthropic + SpaceX 6 May 2026
 
Anthropic takes all of SpaceX's Colossus 1 — 300 MW, 220K Nvidia GPUs, plus orbital data-center exploration.
 
Anthropic gets exclusive access to all AI capacity at SpaceX's Colossus 1 facility in Memphis — over 300 MW and 220,000+ Nvidia H100/H200/GB200 GPUs coming online within the month. The agreement also includes joint exploration of orbital data-center infrastructure for "multiple gigawatts of orbital AI compute." This is the strange-bedfellows story of the week: SpaceX is supplying Anthropic compute while Musk simultaneously sues OpenAI and the Trump administration excludes Anthropic from DoD work. Money over politics at the gigawatt level. Anthropic also raised Claude usage limits for Pro/Max/Team/Enterprise — likely funded by this capacity coming online.
Read more: Anthropic primary · Bloomberg · CNBC.

Anthropic JV 4 May 2026
 
Anthropic + Goldman + Blackstone + Hellman & Friedman launch ~$1.5B enterprise services firm — direct shot at McKinsey/Accenture/Deloitte.
 
A standalone AI-native enterprise services firm with Anthropic engineers embedded directly, ~$1.5B committed capital. Backed by General Atlantic, Leonard Green, Apollo, GIC, and Sequoia alongside the founders. Mid-market, PE-owned companies in healthcare, manufacturing, financial services, retail, and real estate are the initial proving ground. The structure converts Goldman/Blackstone's portfolio company access into a distribution channel and Anthropic's engineering depth into delivery capacity. OpenAI launched a parallel JV the same day per TechCrunch — the consulting market just became a proxy battlefield. For DACH operators, watch for European partner equivalents in Q3-Q4.

Macro 1 May 2026
 
Pentagon awards classified-AI deals to 8 Big Tech firms — Anthropic excluded over safety stance.
 
DoD signed agreements with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, Reflection, and one additional firm to deploy AI tools across classified networks (CNN updated headline from 7 to 8 firms). Anthropic was excluded after the Trump administration objected to its insistence on safety guardrails for Pentagon use of AI in warfare. Read against the rest of this week's news, the exclusion looks more like a policy disagreement than a strategic blow — Anthropic's compute, distribution, and revenue base all expanded materially in the same five-day window.

Policy & oversight · 2 stories
US oversight 5 May 2026
 
CAISI signs pre-launch frontier-model testing agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI.
 
The Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI, within Commerce/NIST) extended its pre-deployment evaluation framework to three more frontier labs. Developers will frequently provide CAISI with models that have safeguards reduced or removed, plus access for the interagency TRAINS Taskforce. The framework builds on Anthropic's and OpenAI's 2024 agreements — Anthropic is already inside this regime. Net read: Trump-era oversight is not "fewer rules," it's "different rules, applied through a national-security lens, with the labs given carve-outs Anthropic helped design." CAISI claims 40+ model evaluations completed to date.
Read more: NIST/CAISI primary · CNN · Microsoft On the Issues.
 
 
Confidence  ●●●●●  5/5 — NIST primary + multiple corroborations NIST
 
EU policy Early May 2026
 
EU AI Act trilogue stalls — original 2 August 2026 compliance deadline now likely to hold for high-risk Annex III systems.
 
Reform negotiations under the Digital Omnibus for AI delayed after Parliament and Council split on overlap with sectoral regulation. Earlier alignment to push high-risk Annex III systems to 2 December 2027 and Annex I-embedded AI to 2 August 2028 is now uncertain — talks resume next month, but a continued stall means the original 2 August 2026 deadline holds for Annex III. For DACH/EU operators with high-risk classification exposure, planning horizon just got tighter; assume worst case until trilogue concludes.

💡 Editor's view
 
  1. Anthropic's compute pluralism is the structural story. Three different anchor partners — Google TPU ($200B), SpaceX Nvidia (Colossus 1, 300 MW), AWS (existing) — plus Goldman/Blackstone for go-to-market and CAISI inside oversight. Either this is brilliant supplier diversification at the gigawatt scale or it's panic-buying capacity. The fact that the SpaceX deal got priced in days after the Pentagon exclusion makes the diversification read more plausible. Watch unit economics in the next earnings cycle to see which.
  2. The consulting market is now contested AI infrastructure. Anthropic + Goldman/Blackstone vs. OpenAI's parallel JV vs. McKinsey/Accenture/Deloitte. Mid-market PE-owned companies are the wedge. The structural insight: this is not a software product, it's a labor-arbitrage product — engineers embedded in client ops at consulting margins. For DACH operators, watch when this lands in Europe; the partner shape will tell you whether to engage or wait.
  3. CAISI is the real US AI policy. Pre-launch national-security testing of frontier models, with safeguards reduced for evaluation, run through Commerce/NIST. This is the regulatory architecture the EU AI Act wishes it had. Anthropic and OpenAI signed in 2024; Google/MS/xAI are now in. Net effect: every frontier US model is gated through one federal evaluator before release. Less press than the Pentagon shutout, but the load-bearing piece of US oversight in 2026.
Wildcard worth naming: orbital data centers. Anthropic + SpaceX explicitly mentioned exploring "multiple gigawatts of orbital AI compute." Between Starlink-scale satellite buses and HBM-class GPU power draw, this is genuinely speculative — but it's the second time in a month a serious US compute deal has included a space option. Worth tracking as a structural marker of where AI infra is being pushed when terrestrial siting / power gets harder.
