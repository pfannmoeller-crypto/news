"When AI builds itself" — and the giddy numbers behind it

The Anthropic Institute published "When AI builds itself," showing with public and internal data that AI is already accelerating AI development, and arguing the world should keep the option to verifiably pause before recursive self-improvement — "which could come sooner than most institutions are prepared for." Anthropic engineers now ship 8x as much code per quarter as in 2021–2025; Claude's success on open-ended problems jumped 50 points to 76% in six months; Mythos Preview hit ~52x on a training-code speedup where a skilled human reaches 4x and 2024's Opus 4 managed 3x. It even improves on researchers who hit a dead end 64% of the time, up from 22% in 2024.

A brake nobody is reaching for

Anthropic admits none of it guarantees runaway recursion yet — picking the right problems is still unproven — but if the curves hold, systems designing their own successors could revolutionize medicine and the economy. That momentum is why it asked rival labs to weigh slowing down, a brake it concedes would be harder to verify than a nuclear site. Everyone else has the throttle pinned: Cognition will foot your bill up to $10M if Devin underdelivers, Mythos reportedly zero-shots medieval Minecraft villages, and OpenAI's Dan Roberts expects the coming months to turn AI on itself until studying it feels like physics.
