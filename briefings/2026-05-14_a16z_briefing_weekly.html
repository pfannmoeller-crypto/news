Anduril raises $5B Series H at a $61B valuation, co-led by Thrive and a16z

Anduril announced a $5 billion Series H round that doubles its valuation to $61 billion, up from $30.5 billion in mid-2025. 2 New Deal May 13 Tessera Labs · $60M led by a16z

Tessera Labs announced $60 million in oversubscribed funding led by Andreessen Horowitz, with a16z partner Seema Amble joining the board. Tessera is building an AI-native platform for enterprise ERP (enterprise resource planning — the core back-office software, e.g. SAP or Oracle, that runs finance, HR and supply-chain operations) transformation, a category that has historically meant multi-year, multi-million-dollar consulting projects. a16z published its own announcement post, with Amble framing the bet as "a genuine category shift — not just making transformation faster, but making it predictable, governed, and continuous." (a16z announcement, BusinessWire, FinSMEs)

Fit with the thesis: same shape as the Ethos bet from the prior week — an AI-native rebuild of a B2B services category whose legacy cost structure was priced for analog, labor-intensive delivery. ERP modernization is a bigger and stickier prize than expert networks: it sits at the center of every large enterprise and the incumbent system-integrators (Accenture, Deloitte) bill billions for exactly this work. The board seat and the $60M lead size mark this as a serious Apps-fund anchor, not a probe. Note the BusinessWire dateline is May 6; broader coverage and the a16z post cluster around May 13, so treat this as a this-window confirmation rather than a brand-new event.

3 New Deal May 14 Stitch · $25M Series A · First GCC deal

a16z leads $25M Series A into Saudi fintech Stitch — its first-ever Gulf investment

Riyadh-based fintech Stitch raised a $25 million Series A led by Andreessen Horowitz, with Arbor Ventures, COTU Ventures, Raed Ventures and SVC participating. The round is notable as a16z's first-ever investment in the GCC (Gulf Cooperation Council — the six-state bloc including Saudi Arabia and the UAE). Founded in 2022 by Mohamed Oueida, Stitch builds a cloud-native operating system for financial institutions covering lending, cards, payments and ledgers; it claims to have processed over $5 billion in transactions in the last six months and grew customers 10x in 2025. Total funding to date is now $35 million. (Wamda, BusinessWire, Zawya)

Strategic read: the geographic first is the story. a16z has historically run a US-concentrated book; a Saudi fintech check signals either genuine appetite for MENA financial infrastructure or a relationship play tied to Gulf sovereign-wealth LPs — both plausible, and not mutually exclusive. Thematically Stitch is the same "rebuild antiquated financial plumbing" thesis that runs through the Fund V framing and the Digital Asset round, just applied to bank core-systems rather than on-chain rails. Worth watching whether this is a one-off or the opening of a deliberate Gulf pipeline.
