Briefing summary
IPO week meets model week: OpenAI files to go public, Anthropic ships Fable 5, and Apple hands Siri's brain to Google
The AI IPO supercycle is no longer a thesis — OpenAI confidentially filed its S-1 on 8 June at roughly $852B post-money, a week after Anthropic's own confidential filing. On the model front, Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 (a public Mythos-class model) on 9 June, Apple's WWDC put a Gemini-powered Siri AI on a billion devices, and Bezos's Prometheus raised $12B at a $41B valuation. Underneath: agent tooling keeps consolidating around MCP, and the credit-based pricing era starts 15 June. Strong week — 10 items at C-Score 4/5 or above.
Frontier models & launches · 4 items
ANTHROPIC  9 Jun
Claude Fable 5 launches — a public Mythos-class model, days after Anthropic's own safety warnings
Anthropic released Claude Fable 5, a Mythos-class model now available to enterprise customers and paid subscribers. The public release is enabled by new safeguards that block responses in high-risk areas (cybersecurity, biology). Anthropic claims >10% gains over Claude Opus 4.8 on some software-engineering and knowledge-work benchmarks. The commercial fine print matters: Fable 5 is included in Pro/Max/Team/Enterprise plans only through 22 June — from 23 June it requires usage credits, part of the broader credit overhaul that starts 15 June. Structural read: the strongest model is moving behind metered pricing within two weeks of launch.

OPENAI  8 Jun
OpenAI files confidentially for IPO at ~$852B post-money — one week after Anthropic
OpenAI submitted a confidential draft S-1 to the SEC on 8 June, following Anthropic's confidential filing on 1 June (which targets a listing near its $965B post-money Series H valuation). OpenAI says timing is undecided and notes "some things are likely easier as a private company." The two leading labs racing to the public markets in the same fortnight — alongside Cerebras (listed 14 May) and SpaceX (priced 11 June at ~$1.77T, the largest IPO in history) — confirms the AI IPO supercycle is on. For the labs this is about funding $100B+/yr compute commitments with public capital, not exit liquidity.

EU  countdown
EU AI Act: 50 days to full applicability (2 August); AI-content labeling Code of Practice due this month
The AI Act becomes fully applicable on 2 August 2026, with transparency rules in force from August. The Commission's Code of Practice for marking and labeling AI-generated content is expected to be finalized in June. The May political agreement to simplify AI rules softened some obligations, but the August date holds — and many member states still haven't appointed their national enforcement regulators. Relevant for both your portfolio companies' marketing-content pipelines and any AI-generated product imagery.

💡 Editor's view
1. Mind the 15 June credit switch. Anthropic's pricing overhaul hits your own tooling first: Fable 5 free in plans only until 22 June, credits after. Decide this week whether your workflows justify metered Fable 5 or whether Opus 4.8 stays the daily driver.

2. The IPO filings change lab behavior. OpenAI and Anthropic both pre-S-1 means quarters, margins and revenue optics now shape model launches and pricing (see point 1 — these are not coincidences). Expect more enterprise distribution deals like OpenAI–Oracle and fewer free-capability giveaways.

3. Apple choosing Gemini is the quiet structural story. The biggest consumer AI surface on earth just became a Google model deployment. Watch whether OpenAI answers with a device/OS move of its own.
Wildcard: Bezos's Prometheus — $12B raised, ~100 researchers poached, aimed at physical-world AI. If anyone can fund a sixth frontier lab from a standing start, it's the man with the rockets.
