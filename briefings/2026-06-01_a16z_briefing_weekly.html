The round is led by CapitalG (Alphabet's independent growth fund), with first-time checks from NVentures (NVIDIA), ServiceNow Ventures, MongoDB Ventures, Snowflake Ventures, Databricks Ventures, AMP PBC and Pace Capital, alongside follow-ons from existing investors Andreessen Horowitz and Menlo Ventures. (OpenRouter, TechCrunch, BusinessWire)

The read on a16z's position: this is a follow-on, not a new lead — a16z and Menlo led the $40M Series A in June 2025 at a ~$547M post-money, so the firm's stake is being marked up roughly 2.4x in twelve months while taking less of the new round than a new entrant. That CapitalG, NVIDIA, and the four cloud/data hyperscalers all wanted in is the harder signal: OpenRouter is being treated as the neutral-router category-definer for inference, the way Stripe was treated for payments — everyone wants a stake, no one wants to be the only large investor. For a16z specifically the position is now sized to matter (5x volume growth on top of the existing mark) without any incremental capital required to defend it. The interesting strategic tension: OpenRouter's neutrality is the product, and a16z is also publicly long Anthropic, OpenAI (via Series G), Mistral and a roster of open-weights bets — that breadth is what makes the routing layer matter, and what could create later conflicts.
