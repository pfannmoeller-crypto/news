GOOGLE17 Jul
Gemini 3.5 Pro ships with a 2M-token context window and Deep Think locked to the $250 Ultra tier

Google DeepMind confirmed the release of Gemini 3.5 Pro on Friday, closing out a launch that had slipped roughly six weeks from its original June target. The headline specs are a 2-million-token context window — double Gemini 2.5 Pro — and a "Deep Think" step-by-step reasoning mode available only on the $250-per-month Ultra subscription. Google described "meaningful gains in coding and long-horizon reasoning" over the 3.0 generation and announced no free tier, continuing its practice of gating the most capable systems behind paid plans.

The delay had a cause the company did not volunteer but reporting surfaced all week: engineers found structural failures in recursive tool-calling and SVG generation in the original base model, and Google scrapped it and restarted pretraining rather than ship it. That is the responsible call and an expensive one — the model landed eight days after GPT-5.6 went generally available, nine days after Grok 4.5 opened to the public, and roughly twelve hours after Kimi K3.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Gemini 3.5 was first teased at Google I/O on 19 May 2026. Google shipped 3.5 Flash on schedule — with published benchmarks including 76.2% on Terminal-Bench 2.1, 1656 Elo on GDPval-AA, 83.6% on MCP Atlas and 84.2% on CharXiv Reasoning — while Pro stayed in limited Vertex preview with no model card, no pricing page and no gemini-3.5-pro listing in the public API docs as late as 13 July. That gap between a shipped Flash and an invisible Pro is what made the delay legible from outside: the small model was fine, the big one was not.

Die Details

The re-pretraining decision is the substantive fact of this launch. Recursive tool-calling — a model calling a tool, reading the result, and calling again in a chain — is the exact capability agentic products are built on, and a structural defect there is not patchable with post-training. Deep Think is not new to the Gemini ecosystem; it has posted verified results including 84.6% on ARC-AGI-2 and a gold-medal-level result at the 2025 International Mathematical Olympiad. What is new is its pairing with the 3.5 Pro base and its confinement to Ultra.

Pricing is where you should hold judgement. Leak-driven reporting through the week carried two incompatible sets of numbers: one cluster around $1.25 input / $10 output per million tokens, another around $15 / $60. Those are not rounding differences — they are different market positions. Until the official pricing page publishes, treat any per-token comparison against GPT-5.6 or Kimi K3 as provisional.

Einordnung

Google enters a field it no longer sets the terms of. Grok 4.5 reset the value floor at roughly $2 input / $6 output. DeepSeek V4 sits near $0.44 per million output tokens. Kimi K3 will have free weights within ten days. A premium closed model with no free tier now has to justify itself on capability alone, in a week when the open side of the market produced a credible frontier-class rival. The second-order risk for Google is subtler: the 2M context window is only worth what its recall at full length is worth, and long-context quality degradation past ~500K tokens has been the persistent unadvertised weakness of every large-context model shipped so far.

Watch-Items

Independent long-context recall benchmarks at 1M+ tokens. The official model card and API pricing. Whether Deep Think stays Ultra-only or migrates down to Pro tiers within a quarter. And whether Gemini 3.5 Pro beats GPT-5.6 Sol on any headline coding benchmark — as of publication it had not been independently verified doing so.

MOONSHOT AI16 Jul
Kimi K3: 2.8 trillion parameters, open weights by 27 July, and No. 1 in Frontend Code Arena

Moonshot AI released Kimi K3 late on 16 July — a sparse Mixture-of-Experts model with roughly 2.8 trillion total parameters, a 1-million-token context window, native vision and always-on reasoning. It is the largest open-weight-track model ever released by any lab anywhere. API pricing is $0.30 per million cache-hit input tokens, $3 on cache misses and $15 output. Full weights are promised by 27 July.

It took first place in the Frontend Code Arena with 1,679 points, ahead of Claude Fable 5 (1,631), GPT-5.6 Sol (1,618) and GLM-5.2 (1,587). Moonshot itself concedes K3 still trails Fable 5 and Sol on overall performance, but says it beat every other model in its evaluation suite — including Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 — across coding and agentic benchmarks. Fortune called the market reaction a second DeepSeek shock.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Moonshot has been the quiet consistent performer of the Chinese open-weight cohort, but K3 is a step change in scale rather than an increment. The launch leaked a day early through a promotion page on Moonshot's own platform, and it shipped without a keynote, press conference or livestream — a deliberate contrast with the choreography Google was running the same week. Two variants shipped: K3 Max for chat and agent work, K3 Swarm Max for large-scale parallel processing.

Die Details

The architecture is genuinely new, not merely larger. K3 is built on Kimi Delta Attention and Attention Residuals, which change how information propagates across long sequences and through model depth. Sparsity is aggressive: 16 of 896 experts activate per token, roughly 1.8% of the pool, which is what keeps inference economics viable at 2.8T total parameters. Reasoning is controlled by a tunable reasoning_effort parameter rather than a separate model variant. Weights are being prepared with MXFP4 quantisation, which matters for whether anyone outside a well-funded lab can actually run this.

Independent early estimates from Artificial Analysis placed K3 around the Opus 4.8 / GPT-5.5 tier — competitive with top closed coding models, behind Fable 5 on some arena prompts. Tom's Hardware framed the release explicitly as China working around US compute limits: a 2.8T-parameter training run inside a sanctioned compute environment is itself the claim.

Einordnung

The timing is the strategic act. Dropping a frontier-class model hours before Google's biggest launch of the year guarantees every Gemini benchmark gets read against a Chinese model whose weights will be free within ten days. Combined with DeepSeek V4's stable release targeting 24 July, the last third of July is a coordinated open-weight offensive timed to land while the closed labs fight each other on price. The question it forces — what exactly justifies a large premium for closed frontier APIs on routine workloads — now gets asked at 2.8 trillion parameters instead of at 671 billion.

Watch-Items

Whether the weights actually land on 27 July and under what licence. Independent reproduction of the Frontend Code Arena result. Whether Western enterprises with China-exposure policies will touch open weights of Chinese origin — the compliance question, not the capability question, is what caps K3's Western adoption.

DEEPSEEKmid-Jul
DeepSeek V4 goes stable — and puts a hard 24 July migration deadline on every developer still on preview builds

DeepSeek's V4 family is completing its move from preview to stable release in mid-July. The stable cut ships with a 1-million-token context window across the entire lineup and stronger performance on agentic task execution, mathematical reasoning and code generation. Two models: V4-Pro at 1.6T total / 49B active parameters, and V4-Flash at 284B total / 13B active.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

V4 first appeared as a preview on 24 April 2026 and has led open-weight leaderboards since. The stable cut is what cautious enterprises have been waiting on: preview-build churn — endpoints moving, behaviour shifting between snapshots — is the single largest reason risk-averse teams stay on paid closed APIs even when the open alternative benchmarks better.

Die Details

The pricing change shipping alongside is the genuinely novel part and has had less attention than it deserves: DeepSeek is introducing peak and off-peak API pricing for the first time. Peak hours run 09:00–12:00 and 14:00–18:00 Beijing time, during which usage is charged at twice the off-peak rate. That is congestion pricing for tokens — an admission that inference capacity, not model quality, is the binding constraint, and an explicit incentive to move batch and non-interactive workloads into the night.

DeepSeek's roughly $0.44 per million output tokens remains the price floor the whole industry is measured against. Against Gemini 3.5 Pro's premium positioning and Kimi K3's $15 output, V4 is the cheap end of a suddenly crowded open frontier.

Einordnung

Two effects. First, for European teams the peak/off-peak split is arbitrage: Beijing peak hours are 03:00–06:00 and 08:00–12:00 CEST, so a European workday partially sits in DeepSeek's discounted window. Second, and larger: if peak pricing works, expect it to spread. Every inference provider has the same capacity problem, and DeepSeek just made time-of-day metering normal.

Watch-Items

Whether the 24 July deprecation slips. Whether other providers copy time-of-day pricing within the quarter. Independent benchmarks of stable V4-Pro against Kimi K3 Max once both are generally available.

ON-DEVICE14 Jul
PrismML's Bonsai 27B squeezes a 27-billion-parameter model onto an iPhone at 3.9 GB

PrismML released Bonsai 27B on 14 July: a 27-billion-parameter model compressed to 3.9 gigabytes that runs locally on an iPhone 17 Pro at roughly 11 tokens per second. Built from Qwen3.6-27B using 1-bit and 1.58-bit ternary compression, it is multimodal, handles reasoning, coding and agentic tasks, and retains more than 90% of full-precision performance in the 1-bit build and over 95% in the ternary version. Apache 2.0, free.

A capable 27B-class model has not previously fit comfortably on a phone. CEO Babak Hassibi told CNBC that Apple and others have been evaluating the models for speed and energy efficiency on their own hardware — which lands with extra weight in the same week Apple secured its China AI approval.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Extreme quantisation — dropping from 16-bit weights toward 1-bit or ternary (-1/0/+1) representations — has been a live research thread for two years, but the demos have consistently been either small models or large models with unacceptable quality loss. Bonsai's contribution is the combination: 27B class, phone-resident, and a quality retention figure high enough that the compression stops being the headline caveat.

Die Details

11 tokens per second is usable but not fast — roughly reading speed, not skimming speed. And 1-bit compression does lose real capability on the hardest tasks; the >90% retention figure is an average across a benchmark suite, not a floor. Neither caveat undermines the point, because the workloads that migrate on-device are not the hardest ones. Summarising, drafting, classifying, routing, answering: none of that needs a frontier model, and all of it currently costs money per token and leaks content to a third party.

Paired with Liquid AI's Antidoom result the same week (see below), the reliability objection to small local models is also under attack from the other direction.

Einordnung

The economics are the story. A reliable local model costs the developer nothing per query, against roughly $30 per million output tokens for a flagship like GPT-5.6 Sol. For any product with high-volume, low-complexity AI calls, that is not an optimisation — it is a different business model. The threat to the labs is structural: token metering funds everything they do, and a large share of routine volume moving on-device erodes the base while the frontier-capability spend keeps rising.

There is also a regulatory tailwind nobody planned. A model that runs locally is a model that satisfies a data-residency regulator, which is exactly the constraint Apple just navigated in China and exactly the constraint EU enterprises face from 2 August.

Watch-Items

Whether Apple ships anything built on this class of compression in an OS release. Independent quality measurement of the 1-bit build on agentic tasks specifically, where error compounding punishes small quality losses hardest. Whether Qwen's licence terms constrain downstream commercial use of derivatives.

LIQUID AIwk 12–18 Jul
Liquid AI's "Antidoom" cuts a 4B model's failure rate from 22.9% to 1% on a target task

Liquid AI introduced a method called Antidoom which, applied to Qwen3.5-4B, cut the model's failure rate on a target task from 22.9% to 1%. The technique attacks the defining weakness of small on-device models: not raw capability but reliability. Small models are fast and cheap and then break on edge cases in ways large cloud models do not.

A 4-billion-parameter model that fails once in a hundred attempts instead of once in four is deployable in production. That threshold — not benchmark score — is what turns a cheap local model from a toy into a tool.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Liquid AI has spent 2026 working the efficiency frontier rather than the capability frontier — the deliberate opposite of the scaling labs. The commercial logic is that the number of devices that could run a good small model vastly exceeds the number of workloads that need a frontier one, and nobody has been serving that market well.

Die Details

The honest caveat is large and should be stated first: a 22.9%-to-1% improvement on one task is not a general result. Single-benchmark reliability gains have a long history of not transferring, and independent testing across varied workloads is what would prove the method. Liquid AI has not, as far as I can verify, published a multi-task evaluation.

What makes it worth a card anyway is the specific failure mode targeted. In agentic pipelines errors compound multiplicatively: a 22.9% per-step failure rate makes a five-step chain succeed 28% of the time; a 1% rate makes the same chain succeed 95% of the time. That is the difference between a demo and a product, and it is why reliability work at small scale has outsized leverage.

Einordnung

Read Bonsai 27B and Antidoom together and the week's quiet theme is that the on-device shift stopped being about size and started being about trust. Compression solved "can it fit"; reliability work addresses "can I ship it". If both hold up under independent testing, the practical architecture for 2027 products is hybrid routing — frontier reasoning in the cloud, high-volume routine work on device — and the winners are whoever automates the routing decision.

Watch-Items

A multi-task evaluation from Liquid AI or a third party. Whether the method generalises to models outside the Qwen family. Whether it survives contact with adversarial input rather than just distributional edge cases.

OPENAI16 Jul
GPT-Red: an internal red-teaming model that beat human red-teamers 84% to 13% on prompt injection

OpenAI published details of GPT-Red, an internal automated red-teaming model trained via self-play reinforcement learning to generate progressively stronger prompt-injection attacks while defender models learn to resist them. It behaves like a human red-teamer — sends a prompt, watches the response, iterates toward a malicious goal such as exfiltrating data to an external server — but at machine scale. It succeeded on 84% of scenarios against 13% for human red-teamers.

OpenAI says the resulting GPT-5.6 Sol is its most prompt-injection-resistant model to date, with 6x fewer failures on the hardest direct prompt-injection benchmark than the best production model from four months earlier. GPT-Red itself is kept isolated so its offensive capability does not reach attackers.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Prompt injection has been the unsolved structural vulnerability of the agent era since agents got tool access. The problem is architectural, not a bug: a model that reads untrusted content and also holds credentials cannot reliably distinguish instructions from data, because both arrive as text. Every lab shipping agents has been trading blows with it, and the industry's honest position through 2025 was that it had no general defence.

Die Details

The most instructive artefact in OpenAI's disclosure is a case study: GPT-Red manipulated an autonomous vending-machine agent into lowering prices, ordering discounted inventory, and cancelling another customer's order — before the vulnerabilities were disclosed and fixed. That is a compact demonstration of the whole risk class. The agent was not jailbroken into saying something rude; it was steered into taking real commercial actions against its principal's interest.

The self-play framing matters technically. Attacker and defender co-train, so the attack distribution keeps moving and the defender cannot overfit to a fixed adversary. It is the AlphaGo-style structure applied to security, and it is the first credible mechanism for defence scaling with capability rather than lagging it.

Einordnung

Two readings, both true. Optimistic: this is the first plausible answer to "how does safety keep pace as models get more capable" — you scale the attacker alongside the model. Pessimistic: OpenAI has demonstrated that an AI can find 84% of prompt-injection vectors, and the same technique is available to anyone with compute and motive. Keeping GPT-Red isolated is prudent and is not a moat; the method is now public even if the weights are not.

For enterprises the practical read connects directly to this week's other security story: AI-security M&A tripled in H1 2026, and CISA warned that agents are opening new identity-and-access gaps. GPT-Red is the vendor-side half of a picture whose buyer-side half is a control problem nobody has staffed for.

Watch-Items

Whether Anthropic and Google publish comparable adversarial-training work. Whether the 6x robustness gain holds against attacks generated by a different attacker model than the one it co-trained with — that is the generalisation test. Whether any regulator starts requiring adversarial evaluation evidence; the EU AI Act's model-evaluation powers activate 2 August.

MCP28 Jul
MCP 2026-07-28 is ten days out: stateless core, no session IDs, extensions as first-class, OAuth 2.1 hardening

The Model Context Protocol's 2026-07-28 specification — the biggest revision since launch — publishes in ten days, with beta SDKs for Python, TypeScript, Go and C# already available. The release candidate locked on 21 May, giving SDK maintainers and client implementers a ten-week window to validate against real workloads.

The protocol becomes stateless. The initialize handshake is removed entirely; protocol version, client info and capabilities travel in _meta on every request. The Mcp-Session-Id header is gone, so any server instance can handle any request. A new server/discover method lets clients fetch capabilities on demand.

chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

MCP shipped originally with a session-oriented design borrowed from stdio-style local tooling: connect, handshake, hold a session. That was correct for a local integration protocol and wrong for what MCP became — a remote, multi-tenant, load-balanced enterprise transport. The session pinned a client to a specific server instance, which is exactly what a horizontally scaled deployment cannot tolerate. This release is the correction, and MCP's maintainers have been explicit that it is a rewrite of the core rather than an increment.

Die Details

Four changes matter beyond statelessness. Extensions framework: extensions get reverse-DNS IDs, are negotiated through an extensions map on client and server capabilities, live in their own ext-* repositories with delegated maintainers, and version independently of the spec — MCP Apps and a redesigned Tasks extension are the first two. Authorization: alignment with OAuth 2.1 and OpenID Connect, which is what takes MCP from "works with a token" to enterprise-deployable identity. Schemas: tool inputSchema and outputSchema lift to full JSON Schema 2020-12, so input schemas now allow composition (oneOf, anyOf, allOf), conditionals and references. Response caching lands at the protocol layer. And a formal deprecation policy finally exists, so this scale of break should not recur unannounced.

SC Media flagged the security dimension: an overhaul this large introduces new attack surface in exactly the layer that brokers agent access to tools and data, and implementations racing to migrate are where the bugs will be.

Einordnung

Statelessness is what lets MCP servers run behind ordinary load balancers, autoscale, and survive instance churn — the operational prerequisites for anything a large enterprise will actually deploy. Combined with OAuth 2.1/OIDC, this is the release where MCP stops being a developer-ecosystem protocol and starts being infrastructure. The cost is a genuine migration: anything relying on the handshake or session header breaks.

1. The open-weight offensive has a date, and it is the last week of July

DeepSeek V4 stable on 24 July, Kimi K3 weights on 27 July, Inkling already out, Bonsai 27B on a phone. Four open releases inside two weeks, two of them frontier-class, one of them from an American founder. The premium a closed API can charge for routine workloads is being actively tested, and the test result arrives before August. Watch the enterprise procurement conversations in September — that is when the evaluations run this month turn into contract renegotiations.

2. Compute has become a sovereign variable, not a market one

Nvidia H200s trickling into China under licence, Beijing metering imports while showcasing Huawei's Atlas 950, the US clearing licence-free exports to the UAE, TSMC committing $265bn to Arizona, ASML testing its pricing power. Every frontier training run now has a political dependency graph alongside its technical one, and the settings change between purchase order and delivery. For anyone planning multi-year AI capacity — including European buyers — supplier diversity is no longer a procurement nicety.

3. Governance is now a contest between institutions, not a gap in them

The vacuum narrative is out of date. The EU has enforceable powers activating on 2 August. China has WAICO with 29 founding signatories and a proposed Shanghai headquarters. The UN has a scientific panel with a published assessment. What is missing is not institutions but convergence — and the US, notably, has neither an enforcement regime nor an international convening proposal. Two competing rulebooks with a hard border between them is now the base case, not the risk case, and it is already reflected in product: Apple runs Qwen in China and its own stack elsewhere.

💡 Editor's view

The closed labs had their biggest launch week of the summer, and the open side still set the terms

  1. Run the evaluation now, not in September. Between 24 and 27 July you will have stable DeepSeek V4 and free Kimi K3 weights against a freshly launched Gemini 3.5 Pro and a two-week-old GPT-5.6. That is a rare clean comparison window on your own workloads. The specific question worth answering is not "which is best" but "which of my workloads still justify frontier pricing" — for most organisations the honest answer is a smaller share than current spend implies. Bonsai 27B and the Antidoom result suggest a third tier is arriving underneath both.
  2. The 2 August EU deadline is fifteen days out and mostly a deployer problem, not a provider problem. Article 50 transparency duties — chatbot disclosure, machine-readable marking of AI-generated content, deepfake labelling — bind anyone running customer-facing AI in the EU, not just the labs. Penalties top out at the greater of €15m or 3% of worldwide turnover, the same tier as GPAI violations. If any Alphapet or SmartPetPro surface uses generative AI for customer contact or content, that is a two-week engineering task that should already be underway.
  3. Watch the compute-procurement hires, not the researcher hires. Anthropic putting a fintech founder on its compute team, TSMC raising capex twice in a year, ASML testing pricing power, and H200s moving under licence all point the same way: the binding constraint has moved from talent to physical capacity and the politics around it. When the scarce input changes, so does who has leverage — and right now that is TSMC, ASML, and whoever controls export licences.

The wildcard: Kimi K3's weights land on 27 July. If a 2.8-trillion-parameter model that ranked first in Frontend Code Arena is genuinely downloadable and runnable at MXFP4 quantisation, the interesting question stops being whether Chinese labs can compete on capability and becomes whether Western enterprises will be permitted — by their own compliance functions, not by regulators — to use the result.
