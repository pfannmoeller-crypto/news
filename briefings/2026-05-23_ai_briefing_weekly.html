Anthropic May 19–20 · Conference
Code with Claude: Managed Agents, "dreaming," and a midlevel-engineer claim
Anthropic's two-day London developer conference ran head-to-head with Google I/O. The substance: Managed Agents on the developer platform — multiagent orchestration, outcomes, webhooks — plus higher Claude Code and Opus API limits. The standout feature is "dreaming": Claude Code agents write notes to themselves about tasks, and a consolidation system finds patterns across tasks so the next agent on the same code starts informed and avoids repeated errors. Anthropic said plainly that Claude is "probably as good as a midlevel engineer" at writing code, while expert engineers stay essential for system design and hard troubleshooting.

OpenAI May 20 · Research
An OpenAI reasoning model resolved an 80-year-old Erdős conjecture
OpenAI said one of its general-purpose reasoning models autonomously disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry that Paul Erdős posed in 1946. Fields Medal winner Tim Gowers called the result "a milestone in AI mathematics." Separate the signal from the headline: it was a general reasoning model, not a specialized math system, and a recognized mathematician validated it publicly — that combination is what makes it a genuine research result rather than a press cycle. Treat the framing as credible while the full proof write-up undergoes peer scrutiny.

EU May 21 · detail update
EU AI Act "Digital Omnibus": high-risk obligations slip to December 2027
The clarifying detail behind the May 7 simplification deal. New timeline: high-risk (Annex III) obligations move from August 2026 to December 2, 2027 — a 16-month delay; product-regulated systems slip to August 2028; national sandboxes to August 2027. Scope narrows — AI used solely for user assistance or performance optimization no longer auto-counts as a safety component. Two new prohibitions take effect December 2, 2026: AI-generated non-consensual intimate material and CSAM. Operational read for DACH operators: the AI-content-labeling/transparency deadline (Dec 2, 2026) is the one to plan against; the heavier high-risk classification work just bought a year of runway.

💡 Editor's view
1. The whole field moved to the agent layer in one week. Google (Spark, Antigravity 2.0, Search agents, Managed Agents API), Anthropic (Managed Agents, dreaming), OpenAI (Codex on-prem), xAI (Grok Build, Skills), Alibaba (Qwen 3.7's 35-hour autonomy), Microsoft (Agent 365), Cursor and Perplexity — every one shipped agent infrastructure, not a chat upgrade. The chat model is now the floor. The product, the moat and the funding are all in orchestration, memory, governance and deployment.

2. Google closed the agentic gap — the flagship contest is June. Be precise: Gemini 3.5 Flash beating last gen's 3.1 Pro is real, and the agent stack is credible. But Gemini 3.5 Pro vs. Claude Opus 4.7 — the actual flagship question — was explicitly deferred to "next month." June is when the head-to-head happens. Until then the Polymarket AI cluster's ~92% Anthropic read for end-of-May still looks right.

3. The talent war is now the real war — and Karpathy is the proof. An OpenAI co-founder joining Anthropic to use Claude to accelerate Claude's own training is the single most strategically loaded move of the week. With Anthropic's run-rate revenue reportedly past $30B and an OpenAI IPO filing imminent, the two leaders are converging on the same scarce input: a few hundred researchers. Watch where the next marquee names land.

4. Two structural splits hardened. Open vs. closed: Meta is pulling its flagship work closed (Muse Spark) while cutting 8,000 staff, leaving DeepSeek V4 and Qwen as the credible open-weight tier — both Chinese. And US vs. EU regulation: the White House postponed its frontier-model order the same week the EU's high-risk timeline detail firmed up. The regulatory map is now genuinely divergent, which matters for any DACH deployment decision.

5. NVIDIA quietly de-risked the whole cycle. Hyperscalers down to ~50% of Data Center revenue means the compute build-out no longer depends on four buyers' capex discipline — sovereign and enterprise demand is now load-bearing. Plus an $80B buyback. The infrastructure cycle is more durable than a year ago, and harder to call a top on.

Wildcard: the calendar doesn't slow down. Microsoft Build (June 2–3) and Gemini 3.5 Pro both land in the next two weeks, and Apple's WWDC follows in June. If you only act on one thing from this edition: the agent-layer shift is now unambiguous — it is worth deciding deliberately which agent stack the pet-food operations standardize on, rather than defaulting.
