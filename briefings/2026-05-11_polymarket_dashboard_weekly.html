trending_upImproving — first material upward repricing in months

The Iran complex moved green for the first time in this dashboard's history. Nuclear deal by May 31 climbed from 14% to 21% Yes; June 30 from 25% to 34%; before-2027 from 50.5% to 60% — crossing the coin-flip line on a long-horizon market is a structural shift, not noise. The driver: reporting that Washington and Tehran are "closing in on a one-page memorandum" via Pakistani mediation. The MoU framework would declare an end to the war, open a 30-day window for detailed nuclear talks, lift US sanctions, release frozen Iranian funds, and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. The single live sticking point: duration of enrichment moratorium — Iran proposed 5 years, US demanded 20, sources point to a 12–15 year landing.

Caveat: on May 10 Trump publicly called Iran's latest response "totally unacceptable," which is the volatility signal embedded in why May 31 only got to 21% rather than 35%+. The crowd is reading "deal pathway exists, but May timeline tight." The before-2027 market at 60% is the cleaner number — it says the crowd believes the 2026 endgame for Iran nuclear is now more likely than the war-continues outcome. This is the meaningful repricing of the master variable.

Russia's strikes on Ukrainian energy infrastructure resume on May 12; Moscow's maximalist demands (Ukrainian neutrality, territorial recognition) remain. Read the binary ceasefire markets as resolved-positive but trade the peace-deal market as the genuine settlement signal — and that one barely moved.
