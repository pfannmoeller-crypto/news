insightsThe meta-meta read

The hawkish lock-in is real and self-reinforcing; AI keeps decoupling; Alphabet is the bridge between worlds

The macro story has firmed, not flipped. Last week the read was "Iran is the master variable, but the Fed is now co-equal." This week the Fed lock-in deepens: 0 cuts at 59%, Warsh adding the second hawkish lock, Bitcoin's risk-on share collapsing 11 points in confirmation. The macro complex (Iran, Ukraine, Fed, politics, risk) now has a structural ceiling that requires both Iran de-escalation AND softer inflation to break. Neither is on the near horizon. This is the regime, not a phase.

The Bitcoin retreat is the key under-the-radar signal. Last week's Gold/BTC convergence (47% vs 39%, gap 8pp) reversed to 47% vs 28% (gap 19pp). The crowd briefly priced "alternative stores of value broadly," then the hawkish Fed lock-in plus Warsh appointment pulled risk-on appetite back to defensive-only. If BTC slips below 25% next week, the regime is unambiguously defensive. If it recovers to 35%+, treat it as noise.

The AI cluster's decoupling is now a documented pattern, not a one-week event. Anthropic at 77.5% for May, 87% to be valued higher than OpenAI in 2026, $500B+ at 97%. Hawkish rates, hot inflation, Hormuz risk — none of it touches the AI cluster's pricing. AI runs on product cycles, benchmark scores, and capital-formation narratives, not on real rates or geopolitics. This decoupling has implications for portfolio construction: AI-pure-play exposure is essentially uncorrelated with the macro book.

The architectural read — updated and reinforced. Anthropic owns the model layer (locked in at ~78%). NVDA owns the infrastructure layer near-term (78.7% for May) but the December market is a coin flip (54%). Alphabet is the only entity in both layers — Gemini in models, Cloud in infrastructure — and the crowd just repriced its December largest-cap probability up 15 points to 35.5%. Alphabet is also the bridge between the AI cluster and the macro cluster (ad revenue exposure to consumer demand + AI exposure via Gemini/Cloud). The single highest-information events in the next 7 days: April PCE revision (tests Fed thesis), May NFP (tests recession thesis), and any Anthropic Claude Mythos GA announcement (tests AI-decoupling thesis).

Industry context worth noting: The SEC delayed prediction-market ETF launches (Bitwise, Roundhill, GraniteShares) this week pending more disclosure clarity. Combined Polymarket+Kalshi April volume hit ~$24B, with Kalshi at 62% share (sports-driven, ~72% of its volume). Hyperliquid entered the prediction-market space and its first Bitcoin outcome did 3× the volume of equivalent Polymarket+Kalshi markets combined. This matters for our data quality going forward: more venues means deeper liquidity but also more fragmentation.
