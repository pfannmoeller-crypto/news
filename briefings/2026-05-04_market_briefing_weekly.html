Fed

Fed holds 3.50–3.75% in an 8–4 split — Powell's likely last meeting

FOMC (Federal Open Market Committee) voted 28–29 Apr to keep the benchmark range at 3.50–3.75%. The 8–4 dissent was unusually wide, with members split on persistent inflation vs. growth risk. The DOJ said it would halt its criminal probe of Powell, clearing the path for Senate confirmation of nominee Kevin Warsh on 15 May. Markets are now trading the regime question more than the next meeting.

Oil Geopolitics

The week-ahead opened with a shock. The US said it destroyed six Iranian small boats in response to attacks on commercial vessels; the UAE reported missile/drone strikes. Editor's view

5 takeaways
  1. Two regimes collided in five trading days. Through Friday, the tape was a clean earnings-momentum story: 84% beat rate, S&P 7,230 record, Apple services record, Alphabet cloud +63%. By Monday open it was a Hormuz oil-shock story: Brent +5.8% to $114, 30-year yield through 5%. Both are real. Position for which one dominates by Friday's NFP (nonfarm payrolls).
  2. The market is grading AI capex on monetization, not faith. Alphabet's Cloud number got rewarded. Meta's $125–145B capex guide without a comparable revenue line got punished. AMD on Tuesday is the next test of the same screening rule.
  3. Central banks are all on a hawkish-hold. Fed 3.50–3.75% (8–4 dissent). ECB 2.00% deposit (unanimous). BoE 3.75% (8–1). All three flagged inflation risk over growth risk. With Hormuz now adding an oil-driven reflation pulse, the cuts the market was sniffing at look further away than they did a week ago.
  4. Pet-food portfolio read. Hormuz-driven energy and freight costs feed directly into DACH/EU input costs (rendering, drying, wet-line steam, ocean freight on protein imports). Watch Dutch TTF gas (the European wholesale gas benchmark) and diesel through the week — see commodity briefing for Verfügbarkeit and price detail. The tail-risk scenario is sustained Brent above $110 forcing a second leg of cost pass-through into 2H.
  5. What I'd actually do this week. NFP Friday is the macro pivot. If payrolls come in soft (under 50k) into rising oil, that's the recession-via-energy script — expect a duration-led equity wobble. If payrolls surprise high (over 100k) plus oil sticky high, the back end keeps grinding higher and growth/quality leadership widens further over rate-sensitives.
