6 Controversy May 5 Andreessen "custom prompt" · X

Andreessen's "current AI custom prompt" goes viral — for the wrong reasons

On Monday, May 5, Marc Andreessen posted what he called his "current AI custom prompt" on X. The prompt instructs the model to be "provocative, aggressive, argumentative, and pointed" and to "never hallucinate or make anything up." Within 24 hours, the post had drawn substantial criticism from AI researchers and engineers — most notably Gary Marcus (NYU emeritus) and Zach Tratar (Notion AI engineering lead) — who pointed out that telling an LLM not to hallucinate doesn't work, because hallucinations are an architectural property of the model, not a behavior the system can simply be told to suppress. (DNyuz, Futurism)

The criticism cascaded across mainstream and tech press over the week. The substantive critique is technical: the prompt reveals a category error about how transformer models work. The narrative critique is uglier — Andreessen is positioning himself as a leading AI policy voice (PCAST, DOGE, Leading the Future PAC) while publicly demonstrating limited operational fluency with the technology. (Cybernews, Techloy)

Strategic cost: limited in the short term — LP and portfolio relationships don't bend on a meme cycle. Longer-term risk is that "Andreessen doesn't actually understand AI" becomes a sticky meta-narrative that complicates the firm's policy positioning. Worth tracking whether the firm tightens his public posture.
