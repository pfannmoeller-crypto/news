Ex-partner John O'Farrell's NYT op-ed: "horrified by what my old colleagues are doing"

Former general partner John O'Farrell — the first outside GP a16z ever hired, in 2010, and a 15-year veteran of the firm — published a New York Times op-ed this week attacking his former colleagues over AI policy. Central claim: the Leading the Future Super PAC, spun up by a16z alongside OpenAI's Greg Brockman and Ron Conway, has raised >$125M to intimidate politicians who engage seriously with AI regulation. O'Farrell describes it as putting "AI hype before the common good." This is the second high-profile ex-partner attack in six weeks (June: unnamed ex-partner on CNBC on "political infiltration"). The pattern matters: the firm's investment brand is now inseparable from its political spending, and departing partners are increasingly willing to say so publicly.

AI · Policy2 Jul 2026

OpenAI reportedly pitches Trump administration a 5% stake (~$42.6B)

The Financial Times reported (via CNBC, Axios, Time) on 2 July that Sam Altman has floated giving the US government a 5% equity stake in OpenAI — worth roughly $42.6B at the March 2026 post-money valuation of $852B. Reported counterparties in the discussion: Trump himself, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick. Reporting frames similar approaches to Anthropic, Google and Meta, potentially via a sovereign wealth vehicle. a16z sits on OpenAI's cap table; if it happens, it dilutes every existing holder equally. Very preliminary — no OpenAI or White House confirmation.

Kontext & Vorgeschichte. OpenAI closed $122B at $852B post-money on 31 March 2026 — the largest single private funding round in history. Since then Altman has been under continuous pressure on three fronts: national-security scrutiny of frontier-model exports, the Musk-vs-Altman litigation still working through Delaware, and rising political heat from Chinese open-source models (DeepSeek, Qwen) that are close-to-parity at meaningfully lower inference cost. The 5% pitch — first reported by the Financial Times — reads as Altman offering a stakeholder deal to defuse all three.

Die Details. Mechanics as reported: primary share issuance to a US government vehicle (likely a to-be-created sovereign wealth fund, following the model Trump has repeatedly floated), not a Treasury purchase from existing holders. That means dilution rather than secondary — a16z, Microsoft, Thrive, Khosla and Founders Fund all take proportional writedowns. The FT positioned this as covering Anthropic, Google and Meta as well, but neither Anthropic (which just signed the $19B TeraWulf lease — separate card) nor Google has made any public statement. The two administration figures reported as most engaged are Bessent and Lutnick, with Trump himself in the loop.

Einordnung. For a16z's OpenAI position — sized in the low-billions on paper — the direct financial impact of a 5% dilution is modest. The strategic implication is larger: it converts OpenAI from a private-sector asset into a public-private hybrid whose exit optionality now depends on the political relationship rather than pure market timing. That reframes the entire OpenAI IPO thesis (targeted for September per pipeline trackers). It also complicates the Musk suit — a US government stake would be argued as evidence of unfair state benefit. And it lands the same week Marc Andreessen sits on the Pentagon Defense Policy Board: the a16z portfolio's largest AI position may soon share ownership with the same government the firm's American Dynamism book sells into.

Watch-Items. Whether OpenAI or the White House confirms on-record; whether Musk's legal team files supplemental briefs referencing the pitch; Microsoft's stance (its 49% economic interest makes the mechanics complex); public reaction from a16z partners; Anthropic's response given the same week's TeraWulf lease.

(2) Puts pressure on OpenAI's pipeline economics, since Anthropic just locked in unit economics through 2046. (3) Elevates Polymarket's Anthropic-first IPO probability, which the 24/7 Wall Street piece reads at materially higher than OpenAI's next-six-month probability — meaning the Anthropic S-1 may be the first frontier-lab public reference, not OpenAI. a16z has been publicly bullish on OpenAI going first; that ordering is now contested.

Watch-Items. Anthropic S-1 filing status; whether OpenAI signs a comparable lease in response; TeraWulf stock reaction and any 8-K filing detail; whether Ornn cites the TeraWulf-Anthropic contract in its OCPI methodology.

That's a scenario a16z's LP communications are not currently positioned for.
