ANTHROPIC 13 May 2026
Anthropic launches Claude for Small Business — 15 pre-built agentic workflows and connectors to QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot
A packaged bundle of 15 ready-to-run workflows plus 15 reusable skills, built on Claude Cowork, with connectors to eight tools SMBs already pay for — Intuit QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, Docusign, Google Workspace and Microsoft 365. The workflows target payroll planning, month-end close, invoice chasing and cash-flow forecasting. No extra charge beyond a Claude Team or Enterprise license. The strategic read: having gone after Wall Street last week, Anthropic is now reaching down-market into the 44% of US GDP that small businesses represent — and pairing it with a 10-city free-training tour starting 14 May. This is a market-expansion play, not a capability one.

ANTHROPIC AWS 11 May 2026
Claude Platform on AWS goes GA — the full native Claude API, console and beta features billed through your AWS account
Distinct from Claude on Amazon Bedrock: this gives customers Anthropic's own native platform — managed agents, code execution, web search, prompt caching, Skills and MCP connectors — through existing AWS authentication and billing, with no separate Anthropic account. Notably, the service is operated by Anthropic and customer data is processed outside the AWS security boundary, unlike Bedrock where AWS keeps data-processing control. AWS is the first cloud to offer this. For anyone already on AWS procurement, it removes the main friction reason to not adopt Claude's newest features. Available across 17 regions including Frankfurt, Dublin, Paris and Zurich.

GOOGLE 12 May 2026
Google rebuilds Android around Gemini Intelligence — cross-app agentic tasks, vibe-coded widgets, and a new "Googlebook" laptop
At the virtual Android Show: I/O Edition, Google announced Gemini Intelligence: the assistant can now move across apps, read what's on screen, and complete multi-step tasks — photograph an event flyer and have it found and booked on Expedia, or turn an on-screen grocery list into a filled cart in your shopping app of choice. Rollout starts this summer on select Pixel and Samsung devices. This is the ambient/agentic OS bet — and the timing, just ahead of Apple's expected AI reboot, is not accidental. Note: this is the Android-focused pre-show; the main Gemini model news is expected at Google I/O proper next week.

OPENAI 7 May 2026
ChatGPT adds "Trusted Contact" — an opt-in feature that can alert a nominated person during a self-harm safety concern
Adult ChatGPT users can nominate someone over 18 who is notified, via email or text, if automated monitoring flags a serious suicide-related concern and trained human reviewers confirm it. The contact receives only a general safety note — no transcripts, screenshots or conversation detail — and must explicitly accept the invitation; either party can opt out anytime. Developed with OpenAI's Global Physicians Network, its Expert Council on Well-Being and AI, and the American Psychological Association. Strategically this is OpenAI building a defensible safety posture as emotional-support usage of ChatGPT grows — and it raises the bar competitors will be measured against. Worth a calm internal note if you have any duty-of-care exposure around AI tools.

EU 8 May 2026
AI Act Omnibus: the fine print firms up — Commission opens transparency-guideline consultation, and the content-labelling grace period actually shrinks
A follow-on to last edition's Omnibus VII story, now with detail worth re-planning around. The 11 May edition flagged that high-risk timelines slip; the newer reading shows the package is not uniformly a delay. The grace period for providers to implement transparency solutions for AI-generated content is being cut from 6 months to 3, with a new 2 December 2026 deadline. Regulatory-sandbox setup, by contrast, is pushed to 2 August 2027. On 8 May the Commission opened a consultation on draft transparency guidelines. Net: if you ship synthetic or AI-assisted content into the EU, your labelling obligation arrives sooner, not later — re-check that specific item against the August assumption.
