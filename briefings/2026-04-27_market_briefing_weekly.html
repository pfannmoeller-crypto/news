FED Funds rate 3.50–3.75%

Fed: held in March, signaling one more cut this year

The FOMC left the federal funds rate at 3.50–3.75% for a second consecutive meeting in March. The dot plot still penciled in one cut in 2026 and one in 2027 — but participants emphasized "nimbleness" and noted progress on disinflation has stalled. PCE and Core PCE are now expected to run at 2.7% for 2026 (revised up). One FOMC participant sees overall inflation at 2.75–3.0% this year, returning to the 2% target only in 2027.

JPMorgan now sees the Fed holding the rest of 2026 with the next move being a hike in Q3 2027. The market's "imminent cut" narrative is on thin ice — particularly if PCE prints hot this Wednesday.

OIL +Brent $106 Throughout the week

Oil: the swing variable for both equities and bonds

Brent topped $106/bbl on Friday, having reached toward $120 earlier in the crisis. The Strait of Hormuz remains under "dual blockade" (Iran since 28 Feb, US since 13 Apr). Supply impact: Hormuz crude/NGL/refined-product loadings averaged ~3.8 mb/d in early April vs. ~20 mb/d in February. Per IEA, March's global oil supply drop of 10.1 mb/d is the largest disruption on record.

Asia tracked these swings literally: Friday Asia oil +2% as the second round of US-Iran peace talks fell apart again. Iran-news headlines are now driving sub-day equity moves; any meaningful de-escalation would compress oil and inflate equities; any escalation does the opposite. Holding equity beta into next week without a hedge on this is a discretionary bet, not a default.

Six things to carry into next week

1
The US rally is narrow — Nasdaq vs. Dow tells you everything.

Nasdaq +15% MTD vs. Dow +6% is a 9-point breadth gap. AI/semis and a few mega-caps are doing the work. If you treat the S&P record as a whole-market signal, you're misreading it. The single-stock concentration risk into Wed-Thu is the biggest tactical issue.

2
The AI capex tax is now binding on beats.

Tesla beat EPS and got punished for raising 2026 capex to $25B. Watch Microsoft, Meta, Alphabet, Amazon's Wednesday capex commentary as the swing variable for the rally, not the headline EPS prints. The market is paying for monetization, not for the spend.

3
Oil is the macro override.

Brent above $106, Hormuz under dual blockade, Iran headlines moving Asia equities by 2% in a session. Any Hormuz development trumps the Fed's communication this week. Holding unhedged equity beta into next week without a view on the Iran path is a directional bet on de-escalation — own that decision explicitly or hedge it.

4
Europe is structurally lagging — Germany cut its 2026 GDP in half.

0.5% growth (from 1.0%) is not a footnote. The Stoxx 600's underperformance vs. the S&P is now backed by an actual downgrade, not just sentiment. SAP and Novo are the structural winners; everything else is rangebound. Re-test European equity exposure assumptions — the calendar reset is real.

5
Geopolitics is bleeding into enterprise software.

ServiceNow's 18% drop, with management explicitly citing the Middle East as a subscription headwind, is the first concrete corporate read-through. If Microsoft / Salesforce / Workday say the same thing on their calls, the AI-software premium across the cohort gets re-priced. Watch this signal closely on Wednesday.

6
The Fed cut narrative is on thin ice.

PCE revised up to 2.7%, JPMorgan now flagging a 2027 hike as the next move. If Wednesday's PCE prints hot, the rally loses its rate-cut leg at the same moment the Mag 7 capex narrative is being tested. Two simultaneous downside catalysts in one 24-hour window is the asymmetry to plan around.
