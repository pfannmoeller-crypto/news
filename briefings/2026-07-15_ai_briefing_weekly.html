OpenAI is running the same play as Anthropic's Cowork and Google's Workspace agents: move agentic execution from a dev-shop feature to the default surface for all knowledge work. MCP28 Jul (RC)
MCP 2026-07-28 release candidate firms up — stateless protocol, three deprecations, a lifecycle policy
The Model Context Protocol (MCP) release candidate for the 28 July spec came into focus this week via the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), the body now stewarding the protocol. Headline change: MCP becomes stateless at the protocol layer — requests carry their own protocol version, client info and capabilities, so any server instance can handle any request with no sticky sessions. Application state moves to visible handles (e.g. "basket_id":"bkt_123") instead of hidden session metadata. Three features are deprecated — Roots, Sampling and Logging — and a formal 12-month deprecation window is now policy.
chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

MCP shipped in late 2024 as Anthropic's "USB-C for AI" — a wire protocol between models and tools/data — and every major vendor adopted it through 2025. In 2026 stewardship moved to the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), and the protocol entered its enterprise-hardening phase. The 2026-07-28 spec is the accumulated answer to production complaints: gateway complexity, hidden state, no formal task model, no versioning discipline. Angie Jones, AAIF's VP of Developer Experience, describes the core shift as "becoming stateless at the protocol layer."

Die Details

Statelessness: requests are self-contained, carrying protocol version, client info and capabilities, so "any available server instance" can serve them — no sticky sessions, no shared session store, no gateway deep-packet inspection. Explicit state: application state uses visible handles agents can reason about and log, replacing hidden session metadata. Extensions framework: reverse-DNS identifiers, independent versioning, capability negotiation. New capabilities: MCP Apps (sandboxed HTML interfaces — a tool can return a widget, not just JSON) and Tasks (long-running operations with status tracking). Governance: feature lifecycle states (Active / Deprecated / Removed) with a minimum 12 months between deprecation and removal, and Standards-Track proposals must ship conformance scenarios before reaching Final. Deprecations: Roots (workspace boundaries), Sampling (model completions) and Logging (protocol-level logging) are deprecated, with those concerns pushed to existing external systems like OpenTelemetry. Final spec lands 28 July.

Einordnung

This is the release that makes MCP boringly deployable — statelessness lets a remote MCP server run behind a plain round-robin load balancer, which is exactly what enterprise infra teams have been asking for. The three deprecations are the more interesting signal: MCP is narrowing its scope, handing logging to OpenTelemetry and pruning under-used primitives rather than accreting them. Combined with the 12-month lifecycle policy, the protocol is signalling maturity — it's willing to remove things on a disciplined schedule, which is what integrators need before they build durable procurement on it. MCP Apps is the wildcard: server-rendered UI could reshape what an "AI app" even is.

Watch-Items

Migration pain for integrations relying on Roots/Sampling/Logging. Whether the MCP Apps + Tasks pairing spawns a real agentic-app tier before year-end. Security advisories around server-rendered UIs and long-running tasks. And how fast the major clients ship conformance to the final 28 July spec.

EU AI ACTT-18 days
Commission enforcement powers over GPAI providers switch on 2 August 2026
From 2 August the EU AI Office gains full enforcement over general-purpose AI providers: information requests, model recalls, mitigation orders, and fines up to €15M or 3% of global revenue. GPAI obligations themselves have been in force since August 2025 — 2 August 2026 is when non-compliance can first become materially expensive. In parallel, a "streamlining" regulation approved in late June defers the higher-risk-system deadlines (stand-alone high-risk to Dec 2027, embedded to Aug 2028) and adds a December 2026 prohibition on non-consensual sexual deepfakes.
chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

The EU AI Act entered force in August 2024 with staggered application dates. GPAI provider obligations — transparency, copyright compliance, safety-and-security for systemic-risk models — landed on 2 August 2025. The 2 August 2026 date is when the Commission's enforcement machinery becomes operational for those obligations. Under industry pressure, EU institutions approved a streamlining regulation in late June that pushes the separate, heavier high-risk-system timelines out by 12–18 months while holding the GPAI line firm.

Die Details

GPAI is defined by a compute threshold (models trained above 10²³ floating-point operations, capable of generating text/audio/image/video). Penalties: up to €15M or 3% of global revenue, whichever is higher; the AI Office may request information, order recalls, and mandate mitigations. Signatories of the General-Purpose AI Code of Practice get a presumption of conformity — the list includes Amazon, Anthropic, Google, IBM, Microsoft, OpenAI, Cohere, Mistral and Aleph Alpha; SpaceXAI signed only the Safety & Security Chapter; Meta refused entirely. High-risk deferral: stand-alone high-risk systems now 2 Dec 2027, embedded 2 Aug 2028. New December 2026 prohibitions cover non-consensual sexual deepfakes and related generated content.

Einordnung

Three practical reads. First, 2 August is not a new-law date — obligations already apply — but it is the first date non-compliance can be fined. Second, the streamlining regulation shows the EU blinking on high-risk timelines under industry pressure while holding GPAI, which tells operators where the political energy is. Third, Meta's holdout is the marquee test case: no presumption of conformity means it must prove compliance by "adequate alternative means," and it's simultaneously running the largest compute build-out — an obvious early enforcement target. For anyone selling AI into the EU, the live compliance surface is documentation (training-data summaries, copyright policy, safety testing) now, and content-marking from December.

Watch-Items

The first AI Office information requests after 2 August, and who receives them. Whether Meta files an alternative compliance framework or absorbs the fine risk. How the 3% cap is computed for AI-native firms without conventional revenue lines. And legal challenges to the December deepfake prohibition.

EU AI ACTT-7 days
Transparency Code of Practice signatories close 22 July, 18:00 CEST
Providers and deployers of generative AI systems have until 22 July, 18:00 CEST to submit signatory forms and appear on the initial list published before 2 August. Signing grants a presumption of conformity with Article 50 — the obligation to mark and label AI-generated content — which becomes operational on 2 August. The Transparency Code is separate from, and narrower than, the broader GPAI Code of Practice, and targets Article 50(2)/(4) providers of synthetic audio, image, video or text.
chevron_rightMehr lesen
Kontext & Vorgeschichte

Article 50 — that AI-generated content must be marked and labelled — enters application on 2 August 2026 alongside the GPAI enforcement date. The Transparency Code, published in mid-2026, is the operational voluntary path: sign, implement, and receive a rebuttable presumption of conformity when the Commission or a national authority inquires. The 22 July cutoff exists specifically so the first published signatory list is ready before Article 50 goes live.

Die Details

Deadline: 22 July 2026, 18:00 CEST. Scope: providers of generative AI systems producing synthetic audio, image, video or text, plus deployers under Article 50(2)/(4). Substance: watermarking/provenance signals plus documentation of what the mark is and how it can be verified. The signatory list will be public. It is a distinct instrument from the General-Purpose AI Code of Practice (safety, transparency, copyright chapters, ~26 signatories) — an operator can sign one and not the other. Watermarking is the load-bearing technical primitive, and C2PA-style provenance is the expected implementation pattern.

Einordnung

The clean signal is the ~23 July published list: who signed and who didn't. Non-signature isn't non-compliance, but it shifts the burden onto the operator to prove Article 50 alignment on its own terms — expensive in a formal inquiry. Expect a fortnight scramble of provenance implementations at frontier labs and image/video vendors. The interesting question is text: "sufficient marking" of AI-generated text is far less settled than for images, and national-authority guidance on that will shape a lot of product behaviour.

Watch-Items

The published signatory list around 23 July, and whether Meta signs the narrower Transparency Code despite refusing the broad GPAI Code. C2PA/provenance vendor traction. And any national guidance defining "sufficient marking" for text output.
